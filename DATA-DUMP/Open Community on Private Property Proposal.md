Trial Overview:

Open community using open systems to manage and operate, with no
hierarchy of authority, while compensating for private land use and
establishing limits of private land use. Compensation for land use is
optionally in monetary or labor form. Using a SCRUM process, members
will bid on tasks posted by anyone. To designate a task as benefiting
the group, the group must accept it through consensus.

Initially any task posted by the land owner, will provide 100% credit
towards land lease. Group tasks will provide 1/n parties credit towards
land lease. E.g. If there are 2 parties, the value will be 1/2 or 50%
towards the land lease. A party consists of the members that are all in
a single lease.

Establish:

1.  rental for allocated space.

2.  Renegotiation period (e.g. 12 months) (to compensate for inflation
    and economic circumstance)

3.  Rate for work type

4.  A list of duties and work to be done

5.  An iterative process for bidding on work items

6.  A process for tracking the value of the items completed that go
    towards the monthly rental cost.

7.  Policy and process for missing monthly value quota.

8.  Private land policy that will be outside the limit of open democracy
    for the group. E.g. no

9.  Exit process

Example:

1.  1000 a month for a one acre spot

2.  12 months

3.  15 dollars a month for general labor

4.  Duties value quota example

    a.  6 hours a day @ 15/h = 90 a day

    b.  In 11 days, the 1000 of value will have been created.

    c.  Or 2.5 hours a day for a month

5.  Every day, a list of items is bid on by those interested in sweat
    value

6.  Using a time task tracker app

7.  The difference must carry onto the next month, for a maximum of up
    to two months quota, the difference beyond this must be paid in
    currency.

8.  Can exit at any time with personal possessions on the allotted land
    with no consequences.

This concept can apply to providing a habitation also (trailer, shipping
container house, foldable house, dome tent) at a various rental cost.

# Questions and Thoughts

## Limits of private land ownership

How are private land limits established, so that the majority do not
infringe on the minority land owner, and vice versa. In an ideal world
this would be done through consensus, but what about time sensitive
issues. From a local legal standpoint, most landowners will have final
say, and in a sense be final authority, but then this does not make the
community truly non hierarchy. If it is only when the land owner says
so, then its just a false sense. Maybe this is just the limit to open
communities on non community ownership. To have a truly open community,
the land must be owned by a corp that is governed by the open community,
within the local legal limits. As far as minority land ownership goes,
the local legal limit will always be there, and the landowner will
always reserve the right to do things like impose new rules regarding
the land, or legally force members/parties to vacate. Consensus should
always be the preferred method of dealing with issues and proposals and
the local legal limits be a last resort for irreconcilable issues. In
the case of community ownership, an irreconcilable issue would still
have the same result, as in a member or party can be voted out, or
choose to leave.
