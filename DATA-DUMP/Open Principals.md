The principals are all interdependent as they are consequences of each
other.

## No Legal Owner

No one will own any app/service that is designated Open. There can be
founders, creators, maintainers, contributors, but no individual can
have any legal right to ownership of the app/service. This does not mean
they can not earn income from their creation or contributions (more on
that later)

Though an app/service can be conceived, funded and built through open
services, it does not have to be open itself and so anyone or any group
can create an app/service and still own it, but the app will not be part
of the open system until it follows the principals.

## No Power Hierarchy 

Fully democratized; Any action must be performed by community vote; a
consensus. No one can be designated with any type of power, everyone has
equal power. This results in no hierarchy of power or decision making.

### No Point of Control

As a consequence of No Power Hierarchy, an Open app/service can not be
shut down or modified without consensus.

## No Hidden Data

All data, changes to data and logs are transparent and viewable by all.
Although since there is no default identification, all personal data,
e.g. medical records, will all be kept anonymous. Therefore private data
can never be leaked unless someone gains a hold of your private key
(\~password), which could be a doctor you give permission to for
example, or if someone gains access to where you store your private key.
It is easy to secure a private key.

A consequence of all data from everything being open to all but yet
anonymous, is that the data can be studied by anyone in order to drive
science, make discoveries, predict events, etc. The data however can
never be used for covert or exploitive purposes, since it is available
to all and owned by none.

Encrypted data is also permitted as long as it follows all other
principals. If data is encrypted, it must be encrypted for all, with no
one having the ability to decrypt the data. Data can only be encrypted
by consensus or app/contract that is Open through consensus. Data is
only mutable through consensus.

# Emergent Principals

As a result of the above 3 principals:

## Centralized Dependency

If an app/service is dependent on a centralized app/service, then the
app/service itself is centralized as a consequence and it can not be
Open. Although an app/service can access centralized data, and as long
as it does not depend on that data, it can still be an Open app/service.

## Right to Anonymity

No users will be required to identify themselves and can stay anonymous,
though they can choose to reveal and verify their identity.

## No Individual Voting Power

In order to have decentralized voting, there can be no dependency to any
centralized system, such as a centralized system that issues and manages
government Identification. Therefore voting power can not be tied to
identity. Instead voting power must be developed by the individual
through one or more accounts by providing value to the community. E.g.
an account that contributes value towards a topic gains consumable
voting points. These voting points are consumed when voting.

# Policies

## Private Encrypted Data Access

In certain privacy cases; committee or jury would need to be formed
temporarily in order to assess some private data that could not be Open
without it violating the principle of anonymity. Once a contract is
fulfilled through a consensus, the data privilege is revoked and can
only be regained through consensus again.
