This document will outline a rollout plan for open world.

Initially OW can be pitched as a Non Profit Open Source Crowd Funded
Platform, or Crowd Funding 2.0

Worry free crowdfunding

Features will include:

## MileStone Goals

Projects can have goals. A goal is like a mini crowd funding project,
with its own specification, fund allocation and funding goal. Goals can
be recursive as many time as needed.

Goals must have an order, and funding for a goal cannot be delivered
until either the goal is accepted by the community, or the previous goal
is accepted by the community. The case is dependent on the funding
option at the beggining of the project; Funding will be delivered when a
goal begins, Funding will be delivered when a goal is accepted as
complete.

Projects dont need any goals, but are more likely to get funding with
more goals.

every goal will have a deadline. If a goal passes a deadline, it will
have x amount of time before it goes into breach mode. Once it is in
breach mode, contributors are allowed to withdraw all of their funds.
After double the deadline time, all funds are automatically returned.
The project can be then resumed under new ownership if there is
interset.

## Project Canceled

The project owner loses owner rights to the project, has to release all
rights to all assets to OFer, and the project becomes open or remain
closed.

# Legalities

By using the platform, owners agree to submit all digital assets to the
platform for liaility reasons. None of these assets will be made public
if the project is not cancelled. If the project is cancelled the owner
agrees to accept an ownership loss ranging between 0 to 100% of
ownership.

# SmartContract

## Goal

If previous contract exists and is not marked as complete, this contract
can not be marked as complete.

If contract is not marked as complete, funds cannot be transferred to
project owner

If contract is normal operation, funds cannot be returned

If contract is in breach, funds can be returned

If contract is canceled, funds are all automatically returned to
contributors

A goal is marked as completed when the majority of project participants
vote on completed after the goal is up for review.

The goal can be marked for review and completed if it is not canceled.

## Project

When a goal is canceled, the project is cancelled and the account can no
longer control the project. The platform now owns the assets and the
project is archived.

## Accounts

New accounts cannot vote, upvoting or downvote; no voting of any kind.
New accounts can only comment. When a new account gains enough karma, it
can then up and downvote, eventually they can gain a fractional vote,
and work up towards a full vote by gaining karma. This is to curb fake
accounts.

To gain karma you must gain upvotes on comments you post.

# BlocklChain

## Whats on the blockchain?

Every goal will be its own contract on the blockchain

Every vote, upvote, downvote and comment will be registered on the
blockchain

Account Id

## Whats not on the blockchain?

Account information will not be on the blockchain such as about you,
pictures, any extra information besides account name/id.

Project details such as pictures, name, description.

Goal details such as pictures, title, description

# Support for Closed and Proprietary

The entire purpose of this OFer rollout is as an intermediary for
traditional to OW philosophies. A stepping stone for both understanding
and practice for users.

Initially all projects with Ofer will be closed and proprietary to mimic
existing crowd funding platforms. The only initial features that stand
out will be centered around goals and votes.

Once this is established and proven, the next stepping stone features
and policies towards OW can be introduced organically.

# BrainStorm / Scrap

A change owner vote passing by majority of the platform participants
will give set a new owner and provide control to them.

what if a goal is completed before a fund goal amount is reached? The
project owner can choose to wait before submiting the next goal until
funding for the previous goal is reached.

if contract is canceled, any funds used and unreturned by the owner are
marked as repayable by the owner.

Any repayable funds are paid by any profit made from any future projects
that are owned by the same project owner.

Depending on the circumstances of the project cancelation, the original
owner can retain part ownership or none.
