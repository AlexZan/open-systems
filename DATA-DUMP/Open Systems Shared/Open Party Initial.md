The Open Party is a political party that can be utilized globally to
introduce open principals into current government systems. The Open
Party introduces systems that are usable in today\'s environment and
compatible with most existing representative democracies, while Open
Democracy begins to develop globally online, setting the stage for an
eventual global decentralized government.

Initially the Open Party aims to improve accountability and response
time to citizen issues by creating a reporting and suggestion system
similar to customer service systems used in successful customer facing
organizations today.

The government\'s main purpose is to serve its population, therefore a
mechanism to allow the assessment of its service performance must be
implemented. Without this citizen feedback loop, there could be a
potential government overreach runaway effect.

An assessment mechanism for all representatives, which will allow the
voting public to file reports against any service members, allow
automated computation of performance measures, as well as suggestions
that will be injected into the current democratic system.

# Systems

## Discussion Forum

A place where anyone in the world can discuss any problem or idea. Users
do not need to have an authorized account to post. Posts can be
prioritized, which will make it more likely for the post to be seen
first. [^1]Prioritizing posts requires an authenticated account.
Prioritizing a local post requires a locally authenticated account. E.g
prioritizing a post in Ontario, requires the account to be authorized in
Ontario.

## Reporting & Suggestion System

This system allows authenticated users to make reports against any
service member or department; anyone or anything that has a public page
within the system.

Suggestions will also be very similar to reporting, except that they are
ideas for improving a department, system or process.

Internally both suggestions and reports will be considered as stories
describing something. Stories will be maintained on an immutable leger
and be publicly available at all times, this includes any updates or
changes made to the story by the agent or owner(person who made the
story).

Reports will affect the performance record of each agent and agents must
maintain a performance record in order to bid on stories.

The more priority a suggestion has the more points it will be worth, and
the larger the payout will be when an agent completes it.

Each story must be assigned to an agent within 1 day or any agent
without an active story will receive a performance penalty.

The rest of the features are described in the use case section below.

## Account System

A system that allows users to create anonymous and private blockchain
accounts, which can also be authorized by government ID through a
globally standardized process, locally.

# Departments

Initially departments must be maintained as is, with the slow
introduction of open systems. Initially only the support department
which handles reports and suggestions will be fully utilizing the open
systems and automated management, but eventually this will extend to the
rest of the departments.

The reasoning behind this is to allow for the smooth transition into
open systems from legacy department operation.

Department systems eventually must be designed in such a way to minimize
overhead and management costs, mainly through automation policy and well
designed incentives.

Using a similar process to SCRUM, each team member is able to bid on
ticket items at the start of each day. Completing a ticket as indicated
by the individual submitting the ticket, without any reports, will
result in points being earned equivalent to the bid.

The agent will earn an income equivalent to the points they earned from
their bids. An agent must be active in order to maintain an active
income. The automated performance record also factors into income.

# Principals

Beyond the Open Principals specified in the Open Democracy
documentation, several other principals will be introduced below to
allow for the transition from legacy systems into open systems.

### Leadership Minimal Media Presence and Donation Based Compensation

The party leadership which are responsible for applying open principals
correctly, and ensuring the system is operating smoothly can not take
any compensation beyond donations made only through official and open
blockchain channels.

Unlike traditional parties, leadership is not front facing. This will
separate any media duties from leadership to allow them to focus on the
overview of operation instead. Front-facing departments such as PR will
instead be handled by specific representatives whose duties involve the
public interpreting the system, policies, and changes.

### No Left-Right Political Participation or Identity

The open party does not itself partake in left-right political spectrum
identity. Some problems require left oriented solutions, and some
require right. The open party will support whatever solution is best for
the people regardless of the political orientation of the solution.

### Everything is open for discussion

The open party will utilize an open source discussion forum where anyone
around the world can discuss anything about the open party. If the open
party is elected into power in a locality, then Users of that locality
can weigh in on any decision that needs to be made by the open party,
since all decisions must be openly discussed and debated by the elected
departments.

### Global Solutions

The Open Party is a global solution for any local representative
democratic system. The local and national instances of the open party
can use policies from the global template that will be ever-growing
through the global open discussion forum.

### Automated Management of Departments 

Departments will feature a self managed SCRUM pattern for handling
workload and performance.

### All Decisions must have a debate 

All proceedings and debates must be recorded and documented in an
immutable leger that will always be publicly available.

All decisions must hold recorded debates, between the team members of
corresponding departments. If a debate can not be had because of
emergency time constraints, this must be documented and proven by
leadership. Once the debate concludes, a vote will be taken between the
involved department members on the decision.

### Government is Customer Service for Citizens

The service of citizens should be the main duty of the government.. Any
citizen can submit a support ticket for any issue they are having at any
level of the government that the open party is responsible for. Anyone,
not just a citizen can submit a question or suggestion. Citizens can
proportize questions or suggestions, and the questions and suggestions
with the highest priority will be first in the stack to be assigned to
the proper departments as they will have the greatest value for
completion.

### All government employees, or employees of government funded services must have a public profile.

Any government employee no matter a health official, police officer, lab
technician, rcmp officer will have a public profile and must provide
their id to anyone that asks for it, and any official interaction with
one of these members must provide the id in the interaction receipt.
Citizens can then submit reports to any direct or indirect public
member, as well as see existing reports. Reports will have automatic
disciplinary actions for government members when they pass preset
thresholds, and will affect progression or viability of that government
member.

## Open Democracy and Local Prioritization in Parallel

Local Prioritization is fully compatible with Open Democracy, and either
can be implemented first.

Users can prioritize local posts until open democracy is introduced with
the forum, then users will be able to participate in global affairs
through open democracy, as well as any organization that supports it.

E.g Users can prioritize a local suggestion about changing the speed
limit on a local street if they are locally authenticated in that
locality through their government ID. Once Open Democracy features get
implemented, users can then upvote a suggestion about how taxes should
be handled by any open party, open party instances can then choose to
and when to implement the latest suggestions.

# Phases

Systems must be gradually introduced. The initial phases will be as
follows in the suggested order:

1.  Register all public employees, contractors and departments that may
    have public interaction with public profiles.

2.  Create a blockchain system for accounts that will authorize with
    government issued ID. These accounts will be anonymous and thus
    private, unlocked only by issued token, which no one including the
    government will have access to, keeping all private data secure from
    anyone.

3.  Create a public service department that will process all reports and
    suggestions made for any public profiles with appropriate
    departments. Since the service department is new, it can already
    follow the new automated policies for self management.

4.  Introduce auto management systems into existing departments to
    manage employees using performance ratings

5.  Introduce scrum story process into existing departments to manage
    workload.

6.  Introduce an open debate system.

7.  Establish citizen and representative voting systems and policies
    with open debates

8.  Begin to roll out proposals for citizen and representative voting.

## Reporting

### Create Report

1.  Go to the public account page, submit report action

2.  Describe report, and add any media file for evidence

3.  Receive a report id after 24 hours.

4.  If a report is not assigned within 24 hours, any agent that does not
    have a report assigned will receive a performance hit.

### Solving a Report

1.  Updates must be made every 24 hours by the agent on any progress, or
    a performance hit will be received.

2.  Agents must follow a kanban style process for resolving a report,
    which is published publicly and openly debated, improved through the
    ticket system.

3.  The process initially will be based on the intelligence lifecycle;

    a.  Planning and direction

    b.  Information gathering (Collection)

    c.  Processing

    d.  Analysis and production

    e.  Dissemination and feedback

4.  The citizen that made the report must accept the resolution before
    it is closed

5.  If the report is not accepted as complete by the reporter, they must
    provide feedback on the reasoning.

6.  Anyone can comment on the progress of a report at any time.

7.  If the agent disagrees with the rejection, they must provide
    feedback on their reasoning.

8.  If there is a standoff with no resolution available, then the agent
    must renounce the report and this will result in a performance hit.
    If the agent believes the rejection is unreasonable they can request
    an open assessment by the public. The public will have x time to
    vote yes or no. If it is yes, the report will be labeled as resolved
    and closed and the agent will not take a performance hit and the
    reporter will receive a record of an unreasonable rejection.

### Report with Private Data

1.  Reports can be made that contain private data which will be
    encrypted against a token which only the reporter and assigned agent
    will have access to, with their account tokens.

2.  If the report must be escelated the reporter must decide to allow
    another account token access to it. The reporter can also choose to
    generate a token that makes the data sharable with a link.

3.  The agent always plays a customer service role first above all else,
    and must help the reporter with any questions about the process and
    guide them through the process.

### Report Scaling

1.  If a report takes longer than 24 hours to be submitted for
    acceptance, the system will automatically start the process for a
    new reporting department piecework agent.

2.  If an agent does not have any reports available for work, they can
    at any time choose to renounce their agent status, or wait for
    reports.

### Agent Report Bidding

1.  When a report comes in, agents can bid for the report, in 24 hours
    an algorithm will assign the report to one of the agents, based off
    past performance, and bid amount.

2.  Agent will bid how much time it will take them to complete the
    report. The hourly earning amount of an agent will factor in report
    count and performance, as well as possibly maturity.

3.  When an agent falls below a performance rating threshold, into
    negative, they will no longer be able to accept reports, and must
    wait for the rating to naturally rise out of negative. Courses can
    be made to help with common issues that might result in bad ratings.
