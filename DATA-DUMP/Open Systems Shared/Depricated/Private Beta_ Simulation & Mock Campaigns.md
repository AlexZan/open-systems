# Overview

You received this because you showed interest in Open Funder and helping
out with testing the platform. You might not know exactly what Open
Funder is, or how it works. That's okay. It\'s not like anything before
it, so it will take some getting used to for everyone. Thank you for
showing interest in helping!

This is a democratized crowdfunding platform. Even though its very
different than kickstarter, it tries to solve similar problems of
funding, so Kickstarter or GofundMe are good entry points for beginning
to understand this concept.

Some of the key differences between traditional crowdfunding platforms
and Open Funder are: No one but the community controls the platform, and
thus any decisions. Campaigns must be broken down into steps by its
creator, versus raising all the funds up front for one big goal of
traditional crowd funding. The final and most significant key difference
is that users vote on each step as the campaign owner delivers progress.
Only once the community vote passes on a step, can the funding for the
next step be unlocked.

There are two roles you can play in the simulation. You can play the
role of a campaign owner, who will create and run a campaign, to raise
funds for a business idea, or some other campaign idea. Or you can play
the role of the end user, who will vote and or fund the campaigns that
get listed. You can also do both, which is encouraged in order to stress
test the platform and systems.

If you can not come up with an idea for a campaign, I can help you come
up with one, or its fine if you would just like to be a voter/funder.

On the day the private beta begins. You will be given an account number.
This will allow you to access the web site and platform.

Everything is anonymous. No one needs to know who you are behind your
account number, but you can choose to identify yourself if you like.

The private beta will use **fake** ether, or fake funds. You can request
these fake funds with a future provided link. This will simulate using
an exchange service to purchase ether from another currency such as
canadian dollar. Currently 1 ether is about \$250 CAD.

Once you have some fake ether, you can browse the simulation campaigns
that the other users have made, see one you like, and fund it, post a
comment, reply to comments, etc. Or you can make your own. Every
interaction with the platform costs a very small amount of ether.

# Steps to Making Your Own Campaign

1.  Come up with a title and a paragraph describing your campaign.

2.  Come up with several steps or milestones your campaign must progress
    through, these are called goals in Open Funder. Each goal has a
    title, and a description. Between the two you must describe an
    objective, as objectively as possible. Ideally with criteria that
    defines some deliverable. E.g. title: 3d render of smartphone case.
    Description: there will be a video link with the 3d render showing a
    full 360 spin around of the case.

3.  Then you need to let people know how much funds you need in order to
    complete these, wether its for purchases, your own time,
    contractors, etc.

4.  Next you need to try your best to estimate how long each goal will
    take.

5.  Once you are happy with your campaign, you can publish it, and the
    rest of the users can see it and begin commenting or funding it.

### Some Tips

1.  The more goals and the more detailed your goals are, the more likely
    you are to get funded. The whole idea is to create confidence in the
    backers, if you are vague, and ask for lots of funds up front, this
    creates red flags, and you probably wont reach your funding goals.

2.  Ramp up your goals funding requirement. Maybe make your first step
    have no funding requirement, to show you are willing to do a freebee
    for sweat labour, to show the community you are serious about the
    product. The more goals you deliver, on time, with quality
    deliverables, the more confidence you will inspire, and the more
    funding your next goal can unlock.

3.  This is a beta! Worst case scenario, the system fully resets, and
    all the data is reset (this is not likely but possible in the beta).
    So I suggest writing everything on paper, or in google docs, then
    transferring it to the campaign creation wizard on the platform, so
    that if it does go down, you can put it back up easily.
