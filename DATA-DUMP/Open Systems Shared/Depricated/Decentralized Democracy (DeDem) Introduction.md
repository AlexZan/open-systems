# Why is it needed?

What problems does decentralized democracy (DeDem) solve? The examples
below are not an exhaustive list.

## Decentralized projects without Decentralized development

Currently all projects that are decentralized, still have a centralized
development process and team. There is no way currently for a community
to vote fairly on a feature or direction of a project. The centralized
team makes these decisions. There is no decentralized way to vote on
accepting a new feature or bug fix completed by a community member, the
team must vote for it.

DeDem will allow true decentralized development, where the community
completes features and bug fixes, but also votes on accepting these
features and bug fixes, as well as deciding what features and bug fixes
to prioritize.

## Crowd Funding and exploitation

Currently many crowd funded projects never see the light of day because
the project owners used the funds irresponsibility or there was fraud
involved. With DeDem the community can vote to release funds to the
project owner as they prove they are progressing through established
project delivery dates. If a delivery does not meet the requirements as
voted by the community, the project owner must make the community voted
fixes and try again. Greatly decreasing the odds that a project is
fraudulent or the owner is negligent.

## Direct Democracy

Communities that use DeDem no longer have to elect representatives in
order to vote on their behalf. Community members who have experience and
an interest in the subject being voted on can directly affect the
outcome instead of relying on a representative to have their best
interest.

# Why has it not been done yet?

Up until now, there has been no truly decentralized solution since all
previous decentralized solutions relied on a centralized service
indirectly making it centralized also. For example, to ensure that
community members each have one vote per person, they must prove their
identity. Typically this is done through the use of a government issued
ID.

The issue with this is that the government body that manages and issues
IDs is centralized, and it can also currently be exploited. The
decentralized community that depends on the centralized service for
identification can not directly control improvements to reduce
exploitation for example. Another issue with this is that government
issued ID is not a global solution, but only a national solution, and
not all nations have the same security standards of identity.

To summarize, a system that depends on a centralized system can not be
decentralized. In order for a system to be fully decentralized, all
decision making must be democratic.

# Achieving Decentralized Democracy

Identity plays the largest role in our current ideas of democracy, since
democracy has always been 1 vote per member. E.g member of a
country(citizen), member of club, member of board, etc.

But there is an easy way to exploit this current type of democracy. Many
voters do not understand the gravity of what they are voting on, or the
entirety of who they are voting on, yet they get the same voting ability
as an expert. This makes it very easy to outvote experts, or those who
are more likely to vote in a communities best interest. One can
influence voters to change their vote, even though that vote might be
unknowingly not in everyone\'s best interest. As well, representatives
might vote with self interest instead of community best interest.

The other problem is that identity can be exploited, therefore voters
and votes can be fake, this has happened many times historically and
voter fraud continues to be an ongoing issue.

The solution is to not depend on membership and therefore identity.
Voting power will not be tied to identity. Members will no longer get
one vote just for being a member in a group. Instead voting power will
depend on interactions, both local and global.

This has many positive consequences that will be outlined later.

## Global interaction

Users who create value are rewarded with experience, this is the core
idea of global interaction.

Users can create value by posting content (artwork, idea, solution,
proof of work, etc) to a forum. Other users can vote on the content they
posted, which translates into the experience points the original poster
earns. Users can create value in any type of subject, independent of
location or group, and are rewarded with experience points.

E.g. I post a landscape photo and tag it with #photography. Over several
days, I receive 100 upvotes in #photography. I now have 100 #photography
experience points.

Only tags can be upvoted or downvoted and a user must have experience in
a tag in order to vote. Voting on a tag consumes that experience. E.g.
If I upvote a post's tag with 1 experience, I lose 1 experience.

When the vote count of a tag reaches double that of the count it had
when a user voted, that user will recover their consumed vote. E.g You
have 1 #photography experience and upvote #photography on a post when it
already had 10 votes. You now have 0 #photography experience and can no
longer vote. A few hours later the post's #photography tag reaches 20
votes, and you once again have 1 #photography point.

A user can earn 1 experience in a subject one day after posting with
that subject even if no one upvotes that subject as long as there are no
down votes in that subject. E.g. I post to #photography, and 24 hours
later, there are no upvotes, but also no downvotes, I now earn 1
experience point in #photography.

## Local interaction

A neighbour, customer, club member, etc, can earn interaction points by
interacting with another member or local institution. Local interactions
can prove interaction with a group, general location (anonymous). This
data is saved to a decentralized leger anonymously. By tracking
interactions and time span with a group or a general location, a user
can have more influence over decisions in that group or locality than a
newer user or less involved user. A user that is new to a group or a
location should not have as much influence over decisions as a more
seasoned user.

### Examples

A local community must vote on whether a park gets an upgrade or not. A
user who is on the other side of the world can see the post, and join in
on the discussion, but because the decision will have no impact on them,
they should have no impact on the decision. Because the user who is not
local has no local interaction points, they can have no effect on the
decision.

A club must decide to change its logo to several options. Users who have
interacted with the club more frequently will have greater voting power
over the decision.

# DeDem Introductory Summary

The above was a brief introduction to some of the DeDem concepts.

To summarize, the amount of value someone creates in a world or system,
directly translates to their experience, and thus their voting power; A
proven expert should have more voting power in the subject they are an
expert in.

Also a person should have more impact over a local decision, the more
that decision impacts them; A person's local impact is proportional to a
person\'s local interaction.

# Requirements

DeDem requires a place where users can post about the value they
created; a forum. A system which can store immutable user experience and
post voting data. Local interactions require a system which can store
immutable user interaction and voting data.

## Technical

A blockchain based system is the ideal solution. Storing critical data
on the blockchain such as vote actions, counts, experience and
interactions, while storing non critical data such as post content,
interaction metadata, etc, and storing the hash of the non critical data
with the appropriate critical data in the blockchain.
