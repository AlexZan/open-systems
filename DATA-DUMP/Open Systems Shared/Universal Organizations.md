## Freedom of Sentience Principal

A sentient (Individual) has the freedom to choose how it experiences the
environment, unless that choice affects the experience of another
sentient, without consent.

A sentient has the freedom to choose, unless that choice affects the
choice of another.

## Entity Principal

An entity (Individual or Organization) has the freedom to choose how it
interacts with any other entity while not violating its derived
principal; sentient principal.

## Certifications

A certification defines how an entity is expected to behave with
parameters. An entity has the freedom to choose what certifications it
subscribes to. All certifications are public domain and an entity has
the right to choose to subscribe to any certification.

Any entity can create a certification or propose modifications to any
certification.

A certification is one or more policies. A policy contains defined
functions and parameters of entity behavior.

## The Universal Community: No Universal State

There is only a universal community, but there can be no universal
state, or any entity in control of the universal community. But instead
states can choose what certifications they subscribe to, to define how
they interact with other entities. The universal community debates, and
creates certifications, but can never have any authority over any state.
The universal community is composed of all sentients. The universal
community is not tied to any location, territory or place in space.
Since there is no authority, there is no censorship of any type. Only
certifications which entities choose to subscribe to can censor the
content from and to the universal community.

The universal community is a set of recursive systems which have at
their core the functionality of entity expression, debate, and
evaluation. The system itself is governed by an ever improving set of
certifications created by the community, which inturn improves the
systems themselves. This is the lifecycle of sentient adaptation. As the
environment changes, and as we learn more about the environment, we
adapt through this process while minimizing harmful conflict (reduction
in the violation of the core principles).

Since the universal community can never have a governing body or
authority I suggest that the universal community use a decentralized
form of democracy in order to assess the ideas that help drive the
certification and policy developments. This can be done through open
deliberation. Open democracy is a form of decentralized democracy that
does not require any form of identification. I suggest that it is used
as a starting point for the systems within the universal community.

Since there is no requirement, nor is it possible, to make decisions on
behalf of the community since there is no authority in the universal
community, open democracy will simply be used to assess the value
created in the universal community. The assessed value, by the
community, will then translate into credits for the entity that created
the value, which they can then use to generate a passive income
proportional to the credits they currently have. In order for an entity
to assess the value of another entity, they must give them their own
credits. Essentially this an experienced entity, recognizing the value
another entity created, and providing them with credits which in turn
represents their experience. I propose the credits earned for creating
value are referred to as experience, since that is what they represent.

Experience is not knowledge. An entity can have knowledge, but not
create anything with that knowledge, therefore never creating any value.
This results in a net negative, since there was effort needed in
acquiring the knowledge, but no value generated from that effort.
Experience is then community assessed value created from knowledge and
successful effort.

Experience is tied to specific subjects. An entity can have experience
in #science, or they may have experience in #photography. There is no
general form of experience.

There can be many open systems in the universal community, and how they
choose to interact with each other is up to the members of the systems
community. A system just like the universal community has no physical
binding (no territory). An entity can then subscribe and unsubscribe
from it at any time.
