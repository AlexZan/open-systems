This will be a community based on open principals and systems. Meaning
it will be fully decentralized and governed by decentralized democracy
systems, using a token economy for management and growth.

Step 1:

1.  Acquire land (either group or individual ownership). Land ownership
    can be achieved through NFT spits, or with rental service cost from
    the owner. Split ownership will be much higher upfront cost from
    everyone, while rental will be much lower upfront cost.

2.  Order dome tents or other modest habitations, once they arrive, they
    can be comparatively built. Any debt of service can be in the form
    of a token.

3.  Individuals with building skill can then propose layout plans for
    common areas. These layouts can be debated and voted on in the
    common area.

4.  Individuals with building project management skills can propose
    building schedules for the common areas.

5.  Individuals with building skill can bid on building jobs.

## Token Economy

Members will post tasks that need to be completed, other members can bid
on the tasks (using a similar to scrum process), and the owner of the
task can then accept the bids. Once the task is complete, the owner can
issue tokens by the amount accepted in the bid, or it is automatic
through contract.

The community can post tasks for the community. Through open democracy
(consensus to decentralized vote), the community can accept task
proposals or reject them. The same process can be used for accepting
bids. Anyone can bid on a task, and through consensus, the lowest bid
will be automatically selected unless blocked by other members(s). E.g.
they do not think the lowest bid has adequate skill.

In small communities this will probably be discussed verbally first
before interacting with the immutable system for bids, accepting
completion (quality assurance) and token exchange.

The tokens can be used for any service listed by any member. E.g. A user
lists a tool rental, spray foam machine for x tokens. Another user that
wants to rent the machine must perform some task that the user posts for
equivalent or more tokens. Optionally the owner can specify an insurance
policy contract, in order to state the value of the item, and the amount
the user needs to have in an insurance escrow contract in order to cover
the costs of damage/repair. The owner can also optionally post required
training material to operate the machine, which the user must sign off
on having covered. If they damage the item with something covered in the
training material, they are fully liable for any damages. These can be
templated contracts that the members can write, and select when posting
services/tasks.

This can also apply to private land use. Each time interval (month) a
token amount must be transferred as agreed by the land owner and tenant.
Therefore the tenant must perform tasks for the tokens, or buy them with
other currencies at an established exchange.
