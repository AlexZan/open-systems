By allowing contracts to contain a list of sub contracts ,we can have a
contract network, an ecosystem of pathways driven fully by decentralized
democracy.

A subcontract list will be contained with each event in the contract.
Every condition must have submittable middleware through decentralized
democracy.

When a contract reaches an event point, it will execute a list of
subcontracts. Each subcontract is its own contract and must be driven by
its corresponding policy.

E.g a user will gain experience from votes 24 hours after they have
posted, is the policy, and the sub contract list is executed when the
event of making a post is executed. In this case the specific contract
sets up a timer for 24 hours to perform another action. This event in
turn calls its own sub contract list.

How is this different from just voting in an amendment to contracts? It
allows the base contract to stay the same, thus the address to the
contract stays the same, while allowing for updates at its event points.
If the base contract is changed, then, the address will need to be
changed in some address registry and then redistributed to all peers.
The other major difference is that updates can be made concurrently with
less chance for conflict and greater potential for collaboration.
