# Information in Social Political Systems

## Humans Exploit

Humans are the animal with the greatest cognitive ability to model and
understand the world around us. We model the world around us so that we
may learn how to exploit it to our advantage. This is human nature.

There is no perfect system of collaboration or government. All social
political systems have their set of issues.

Any political system can be abused or exploited. This will be the case
for many of the ideas proposed in these documents. The only way to
mitigate this is through open discourse. To point out trends and
exploits from those who stand to gain from its abuse, to stop these
issues before they become systemic, to update the system or change it
before more damage is done. If something can be exploited, humans will
find a way to and it should always be expected.

Social media has been a powerful platform that for the first time
allowed the greatest reach for discussion, crossing national and
cultural bounds. As time went on, social media companies and
institutions which were influenced or driven by the agendas of the
powerful began to successfully censor any opposition to their views,
which further supported exploitation. We are at a point in history,
currently, where it is possible to engineer a global social opinion that
gets disseminated through social media, through means which seem natural
and where the majority takes ownership of these opinions as their own,
defending them as their own.

## Ideas can be Dangerous without Debate

Without a healthy and balanced opposition to popular opinions, with
meaningful checks and balances, ideas can lead to unhinged movements,
and may cause a great deal of damage. An idea alone is not dangerous,
but an idea that is acted on, without first being understood and debated
can be dangerous. Anyone who expresses that an idea as dangerous is in
fact expressing that they stand to gain from the idea being censored.

All ideas should go through a life cycle of first being proposed,
applied on a small scale, then reviewed, before they are applied on a
larger scale. This process must be open to all forms of discussion, by
all, especially to critique, because it is only through critique that
problems can be addressed and improvements be made.

As of late, due to global corporatism behavior, censorship has become
more organized. Manufactured cancel campaigns, orchestrated or
influenced by some of the most powerful institutions that should be
protecting the public instead of silencing critics that do not fit the
current agenda have become a common occurrence.

Most of those issues have one thing in common; deception.

## Organized Deception

We now have the ability with social media to empower every individual,
globally, to provide the most creative solutions to problems, but,
instead we are using social media in very strange ways, in very
exploitative ways to empower just the few while keeping the majority of
individuals as consumers of this pattern, this lifestyle, all while
convincing consumers they enjoy it, that it gives them purpose of a
sense of belonging. We have weaponized social media.

To reiterate a previous point; this is human nature; to exploit a
resource for gain, even if that resource is human beings, because if one
individual does not, someone else will. To be more specific, It is a
property of the current state of intelligence we are in at this time in
history. Intelligence has the ability to model the world or environment
truthfully in the pursuit of knowledge, the curiosity of exploration;
the gain of all, or it has the ability to model the world fictitiously
in the pursuit of individual gain from deception and thus loss in
others. The latter maintains the zero sum gain of current state
competition, utilizing deception as a tool, the former enhances our
ability to realize a greater potential.

If we can reach a point where this is collectively understood, we can
then begin to put effort into systems that support the former and not
the latter. We have already been attempting to do this for thousands of
years. It is much easier to steal your means of living than to earn it,
but we have systems in place, such as police, morality through religion,
ethics through culture, to mitigate those exploits. But these systems
themselves are susceptible to the same type of exploitation. The police
force can create an internal culture of exploitation and abuse just as
institutions of religion can, just as governments can mandate efforts to
alter broad culture and ethics to suit a political agenda. The systems
in place to stop abuses from happening can also be abused and so it goes
on, all the way to the top of our current social and political systems.

To circle back to the core point; the only way to mitigate this is
through open discussion. These exploits and abuses are only possible
through deception; through the cover up of events and information or the
campaigns of manufactured information. A formalized system for open
discussion that makes it difficult for deception and exploitation is
required.

## Open Forum

Our focus should then be a system that focuses on uncompromisable open
discussion. A system that recognizes that its own function might be
exploited and abused, and does not deny that possibility, but allows
open discussion about its own potential flaws so that they are
consistently addressed. Transparency being one of the key principles a
system should have to be open. By having all interactions with an open
system maintain transparency, there is no opportunity for hidden
interactions, transactions or control; all system actions can be
validated by anyone, through a record of interaction. There is no hidden
functionality.

Shortly, this document will propose the other key principles that open
systems should include initially. This list is not set, it will change,
grow, shrink, with time, with the environment, and the only thing that
enables it to do so, is open discussion. The golden rule is to guarantee
uncompromised open discussion, and thus, freedom of speech should be
held to the highest regard in any social political system by its
members.

This list of open principals can be thought of as a constitution for
systems that are considered to be open. Constitutions should be able to
change, they exist to match the current environment, but the environment
changes, and so should a constitution, but it should not be easy.
Initially to the time required to conclude on the challenge an idea put
forth in a constitution, it should take the same amount of time the idea
has been in place for. Constitutions should be the building blocks for
all other policies and functionality, making them the hardest document
or contract to change. More specifications can be found in the
pertaining constitution documents.

## Open Principals

These are the remaining initial open principals. To provide some

### Decentralized

There should be no central point of control in its operation, so that it
can not be controlled by the interests of the few, or censored by the
central point of control when it is in conflict with those that control
it. The development and maintenance itself needs to be decentralized,
otherwise the development teams may be compromised and forced to violate
these principals. E.g. centralized censorship access. Decentralization
requires all actions to be transparent since they will be occurring
publicly everywhere.

### Transparent

All interactions with the system should be visible to all. There should
be no hidden functionality that allows deceptive practices. e.g. shadow
banning, biased training data in AI.

Transparency requires anonymity so that it can maintain rights to
privacy.

### Anonymous

Anonymity should be the default with the option for users to identify
themselves for those that wish it. Anonymity allows the discussion to
focus on what is being said rather than who said it. Anonymity protects
individuals for expressing views that critique potential abuses of those
in power. Anonymity depends on the system being decentralized, since no
central control can force encrypted information out of the system as
they could with a central system.

## Knowledge Network Introduction

Discussions and debates can often be duplicated efforts. Many of the
debates going on in the world already have occurred elsewhere, and have
achieved some level of a conclusion. While having the debate multiple
times might lead to some new creative insight, it does become exhaustive
at some point, with diminishing returns. To improve this, we can start
with an existing discussion as a foundation to build upon.

The next system is a network of knowledge. Knowledge is a belief we
consider true through some form of consensus, often scientific, and
always through rational reasoning. It is a network because it will allow
users to relate ideas to other ideas through a weight which supports or
denies the linked idea or evidence, creating a network structure of
organization.

For example, let\'s imagine some users agree with and post the idea "the
world is flat". Many other users would link several ideas creating a
disproving or negative relation, such as, "Coriolis force" which
describes rotation matching observed reality of earth that requires the
mass of a sphere not a disc. Since "Coriolis force" is a much older
concept, tried and tested for much longer, it would have a much greater
"positive" or "most likely true" weight than flat earth, which would
strengthen the "negative" or "disproving" weight of its relation to the
flat earth concept. Users could link several other concepts, evidence
which would severely disprove the flat earth concept. Coriolis Force
itself would have very strong theories backing it up providing it even
more weight or strength.

Maybe in the more distant future some new concepts such as 3 dimensions
emerging from the projection of 2 dimensions might explain that it
wasn't all that wrong from another perspective, and that could change
its overall weight. The general idea is that it is impossible to achieve
\"the truth\" or absolute truth, we can only approach truth over time,
through discussion, and that discussion on specific subjects can be
greatly enhanced if it is formalized through a network of existing
knowledge. The Knowledge Network in a sense also formalizes the concept
of marketplace of ideas.

Just like the open forum, the knowledge network would use the open
principals so as to not have its integrity compromised so that it can
become an effective reference to the closest version of the truth we
have. It is important to trust a knowledge reference that no one has
altered it deceptively in order to enhance an agenda or belief that has
not met the test of debate and time. This is why fact checker systems in
use today are flawed.

The details and specification for the knowledge network system and

### Decentralized Consensus and Voting

In order to assess value on posts in the open forum, or define the
support of an idea by another on the knowledge network, through a
decentralized and democratic process, we need to be able to vote through
decentralized means.

Currently voting is generally done with one vote per citizen or member.
But since users are anonymous, anyone could make multiple accounts and
exploit the vote count by voting with multiple accounts.

If we verify users with government issued identification we depend on a
system that is centralized and any decentralized system that depends on
a central system is no longer decentralized, violating the open
principals. The other issue with government id is that authentication is
inconsistent, in different organizations or nations. E.g. it is easier
to create fake id in some parts. This is not a new problem introduced
here; voting fraud has been an issue throughout history.

We could instead elect representatives that decide the relation between
ideas, but now we have the same issue of voting on the representatives
as we did on directly voting on ideas. Furthermore we have introduced
the risk of abuse as there is with any role of power through
representation, where a representative can promote an idea more than
another if it suits their personal interests more than the pursuit of
truth.

We are back to the problem of identifying users to a single human
individual so that multiple accounts can not be faked. While it is
possible to do this, there will be a proposal such as this for another
system that requires it, the knowledge network and forum do not need
this level of complexity. The simplest solution is to remove the need
for identity entirely.

## Experiential Democracy: Identity-less Voting

As a consequence of removing the need for identity, we need to remove
the ability of one vote per member to remove the incentive for making
multiple accounts for multiple votes (as described earlier).

The experiential voting system is not being proposed to replace the
existing system for representative democracy and the electoral process.
This system is simply used to assess the value of ideas and the
relationship between ideas.

This is a concept being proposed which allows users to vote with the
value they create in specific fields or subjects that they become
experienced in. These are a set of suggestions of how the system should
function initially, but it can quickly change and improve through the
open process.

### Initial Voting Power

With experiential voting, all users start with 0 or no voting power when
they first begin using an open system. Users are not able to vote until
they acquire voting power. To acquire voting power, a user must first
contribute some value to a specific subject. If we imagine the knowledge
network using an open discussion forum to debate ideas and their
relationships, then, one example of contributing value is through
posting a good rebuttal to a debate. When other users think a post to
the forum has value, they simply upvote the post.

### Earning Experience

The open forum is similar to a standard online discussion forum, but
users upvote and downvote subject tags on posts, instead of the post
itself. By contributing value to a subject defined by the hashtag
symbol: #, those votes are converted into experience (voting power) for
that specific subject, for that user.

For example, You have an idea for an app. You post your app idea and tag
it with the subjects; #app. After one day, if no one believes it is a
bad idea and no one down votes the post, you are awarded 1 experience
point in both #app. **Experience is earned in subjects.**

If users upvote your idea in #app after one day, on top of the 1
experience you earn by default, you also earn all the votes made towards
your post. E.g. there were 10 votes in #app. After a day you earn 11
experience in #app. **Votes received are turned into experience
points.**

A user upvotes your app idea with a new tag, for example if it is a
rideshare app idea, but you didn\'t initially tag it with #rideshare, a
user might add #rideshare as a new tag and give you 1 experience in that
subject. **Any subject can be upvoted or downvoted**

Essentially you are contributing value to the subject just by posting an
idea, but you could also be a programmer submitting your code for review
as a post, earning experience in #programming and #app. You could be an
artist posting your #painting. You could be a #mechanic posting a #DIY
video. **Any content can be posted, text, code, links, video, pictures,
etc.**

[If a user just created an account, they can not upvote #app, since they
do not have any experience in #app. **You must have experience in order
to vote.**]{.mark}

When a user votes on a post\'s tag, the experience they voted with is
consumed. E.g. you upvote #app, and you lose 1 experience from your
total experience point. This encourages users to use their experience
effectively on things that matter to them, on things that bring them
value. **Voting power is consumed.**

When a user votes on a tag, and the vote count doubles from the point at
which they voted, they will also earn experience. E.g. User x has 1
experience point in #app and upvotes #app when it had 0 votes, taking it
to 1 vote. Another user upvotes #app and it is now 2 votes, causing user
x to earn back 1 experience point in #app. **Experience points can be
recovered by voting early.**

If you find a post that you think has a great deal of potential, you may
upvote it for as many experience points as you have. E.g. You can upvote
100 points in #app, thinking that this is a great idea, and the post
will easily reach a high number of votes, allowing you to earn back 200
experience points as an investment. **You can upvote with multiple
experience points.**

If a user posts something you believe takes away value in some subject,
users can downvote the post in those tags. E.g. A user makes a post
about making money by deceiving other people, this user could get
downvotes in #ethics. **Users can lose experience by being downvoted.**

**Experience is the currency in the marketplace of ideas.**

### Who Decides Value?

While the answer is already implicit in the application definition
above, everyone is able to decide value, together. Everyone has the
opportunity to have a say. Either through a reply or vote. Since, in
order to vote, you must have experience in a subject you are voting in,
you first need to create some value yourself in a subject through an
original post or reply. The more value you create the more value you
will be able to assess through the acquired voting power of experience.

For example, you are mathematician, but a new user, and you can post the
solution to a problem someone posted, users will upvote you in
#mathematics. Another example, you are a new user, but you find a flaw
in the app idea someone posted and reply a working solution, gaining
experience in #app #development #debug.

It is the same mechanic that has been occurring for thousands of years
between a teacher and students. At some point the student will have the
knowledge to go out and create some value, then they can become the
teacher themselves and assess other students in their journey and so the
pattern goes on. Experiential Democracy formalizes this process with
open standards.

### Fluid Experts and Representation

Since an expert is an individual with a substantial amount of
experience, and thus voting power, an expert in a sense is an elected
representative. Instead of voting on the representative directly, users
vote on the value the expert created, thus empowering the expert to
create even more potential value, or take a larger part in decisions
with the voting power they are given. Voting power can eventually be
used to make decisions with collaborations, policy, etc as will be
explained later in this document.

An important point to make clear is that experts can gain and lose
experience at any time, this results in representation and experts being
fluid. There is no time frame or term of power, it can come and go with
every action, becoming less, or more, or even go into the negative, in a
sense requiring redemption. Each time a representative gains or loses
experience, there is an immutable decentralized record tracing every
action they performed which led to an increase or loss in experience.
The user and their record is anonymous unless the user chooses to
identify themselves. Even though the user is anonymous, the record of
any user can be easily reviewed by anyone, it is public and transparent.

Another important point is that there is no general representation. Each
representative only has voting power in their specific subject fields.
An expert in #psychology does not have voting power in #climate science,
unless of course they have demonstrated value and thus expertise in that
field also.

### Won\'t There be a Great Deal of Useless Posts to Filter Through?

Yes. This is a consequence of true freedom of speech. But since everyone
else is able to assess value, and is incentivized to, there should be no
imbalance in the filtration mechanic; it should scale organically. The
most valuable posts will rise to the top in the respective subjects
which will allow more users to see the post.

Users who have knowledge in a subject and are trying to gain experience
in it will compete with each other to assess value in new posts, through
responses and critique. Posts with high potential value will quickly
rise to the top, and they will gain more attention by users with even
more experience in that subject. The post will receive even higher
quality critique from more expert users, this will continue as the post
rises in assessed value.

For example, If it is a post with great potential value, it might make
it to the top 100 list in a subject, or the top 10, in which case it
will receive critique and analysis from the top experts in that field.

The full details of this application and others can be found in the
respective specification documents.

### What about Harmful Posts?

Harm is subjective, and while a majority can agree on what causes harm,
it is still subjective, and thus censorship should not be objective or
global. Censorship does need to exist, but without compromising freedom
of choice. This is the age old dilemma: by allowing true freedom of
speech you allow harmful content that might hurt the freedom of others.
E.g. a child seeing content they are unable to process in a healthy way
that would give them trauma.

The solution is to allow the viewer or individual responsible
(parent/guardian) for the viewer to decide what they individually would
like to see censored, and it is censored just for them, not everyone
else. If the majority wants to see swear words censored, but a minority
does not, now the minority will not be forced to comply for the benefit
of the majority. This does not cost the majority or the minority, and
allows all parties to maintain freedom of choice at their own risk.

To achieve this, each poster can select certifications when posting, to
describe the expected content they are posting and the viewer. On the
receiving end, viewers can select certifications to filter more specific
content. For example, if the post does not contain a "swear free"
certification, then anyone requiring this certification will not be able
to see the post.

## Certification

An open certification is a set of contracts defined on an immutable
decentralized database through the open process. These contracts define
expected behavior for all entities; human individuals, systems,
organizations.

### Certification Over Censorship

Instead of censoring ideas, individuals, or movements, we can transition
into certifications. These certifications are openly developed by the
open community, using the open process: experiential democracy, forum
and the knowledge network. Anyone can propose a new certification, or
fork (build off) an existing certification. Any individual can subscribe
to any certification.

Users can choose to put their trust in institutions that maintain
certifications which specialize in specific quality control. These
institutions will debate and propose several standards from existing
certifications. This way the individual is not bogged down with the work
of setting up their own ideal certifications, but they can subscribe to
the best standards that describe their ideal online experience, in much
fewer steps.

If a user is not happy with a certification or has found a problem with
it, they can report the bug, propose an improvement, or create their own
or fork a certification, at any time.

This results in an open forum, which can be setup with no
certifications, with posts without any censorship, where users can post
the most harmful things imaginable, but also the most creative and
helpful critique that might otherwise get censored. A user with many
certificates enabled will have much of what they considered harmful
filtered out. The end goal is to maintain full freedom of choice and not
to force protections.

E.g. A children\'s content community/institution will have many
certifications that will automatically filter out any harmful content
for children, as well as the posters must explicitly decide to post
child safe content and abide by all the top childrens content
certifications rules in order to reach a wider target audience.

The control of content is between the poster and the viewer, not the
responsibility of a centralized authority. Viewers can choose how safe
they wish to be, and posters can choose how wide or specific of an
audience they wish to achieve.

### Automatic vs Manual

Certifications can involve filters that automatically filter out data,
or definitions of expected behavior which the poster must contractually
abide by.

An open certificate is a set of rules to filter out data and/or define
expected content/behavior. An automated function (either human coded or
AI driven), will take in data, and filter or perform some other action.
A certification might even stop a poster from using a certification tag
because it detects something invalid in their post which goes against
expected behavior. This allows the poster to make fixes if they wish to
use the certification, or they can post without the tag. A set of human
interpretable rules will set the expectation for behavior from a program
or entity that has that certification. Certifications will be managed in
a marketplace of competing, ever improving certifications.

A certification is not only used to filter out content for the viewer
but to also notify the poster when they violated a certification, before
they post.

In its simplest form a certificate is a promise for an entity
(individual, group) to behave a specified way. This concept of expressed
expected behavior will play a critical role in future systems.

### Certification Abuse

Users can post harmful content with the wrong certifications in some
cases, since selecting the correct certifications is a manual process
and the responsibility of the poser. This might be done with intent or
ignorance. There are many ways to mitigate this. The first line of
defense can be an ever improving AI to auto detect content that does not
match certifications to let the user know they might be posting with the
incorrect certification, as mentioned earlier. The other methods involve
reporting and auditing by the open community which will be covered in
other documents.

### Certificate Institutions 

The continuous debate of certifications in specific fields can lead to
institutions emerging, which will allow the creation of standards;
generally accepted sets of certifications describing how a type of
organization should function and interact with individuals and other
organizations.

This will make it far easier for users to customize their experience and
interactions, without having to dig through potentially thousands of
certifications themselves. They can sort through the most used standards
that match their requirements.

Since these types of institutions emerge to represent the interests of
users, they are susceptible to participating in abuses, such as
promoting a certification that advances their personal interests
unfairly. This is easily mitigated through the open process of
discussing the potential abuse of a certification, or providing evidence
that an institution is developing/using a certification with issues.

## Open Process

We now have described systems to provide the ability to have open
discussion for all with an open forum, the ability to relate ideas to
each other with a knowledge network, to allow for more structured
debates by referencing tried and tested ideas with newer ideas, for the
ability for these systems to function based on a constitution defined
through discussion and debate.

Proposing an idea which might affect the existing function, policy and
constitution of the systems which the discussion takes part in creates a
feedback loop which allows open systems to continually improve and
adapt, while mitigating any risk of damaging changes through a hierarchy
of policies with the constitution sitting at the top, making it the most
difficult to change.

The open process allows a system to change itself through open
discussion and decentralized decision making.

## User Accounts

With purpose, system specifications have been kept to a minimum in this
document. This document is a broad overview of the core systems, and its
purpose is to simply introduce the ecosystem. There are many other
documents that propose specifications, but one specification regarding
user accounts is worth mentioning, since it might be required by some
readers to get a clear picture of how users interact with the systems
and applications.

A user account is composed of two things, a public token, which everyone
can see and it is used to identify your account. The next component is a
secret token which is similar to a secure password. There is no other
information that is required from the user or for the user to identify
themselves, to interact with the systems; There is no name, location, or
any other information required. A user can optionally include any
information they want, such as name, or alias, but a user can post to a
forum with just their token and secret, and other users will see the
post as created anonymously with that public token.

A user does not even need to create an account, they can just load the
app, and post, and an account token and secret will be generated and
locally stored for them which they can retrieve at a later time. There
are several techniques to mitigate abuse such as spam, such as a
referral/invite/sponsor systems and a monetary staking system. These
specifications can be found in system specific specification documents.

## Instances

Since there is no central control to the entire open ecosystem, anyone
is able to start a brand new instance of the ecosystem, containing no
users, no experience, and is customizable in every aspect, including the
initial constitution. This might be useful when an organization, nation,
or anyone decides they want something different than what the current
standard. A new instance will be a completely blank slate with no users,
and so users must choose to join if they see value in this new ecosystem
instance over their current.

There might be several reasons for starting a new instance. Maybe a
nation or group does not have trust towards the current instance, or
they think it has been compromised, or their values just have too large
of a gap, and there exists no certification for compatibility between
the different values yet. Maybe the original instance was corrupted
through abuse, error or breaking changes and the best solution is to
reset it or create a new instance.

This gives the individual the ultimate freedom of choice, to be able to
choose which universal (non local) system they would like to participate
in, and to be able to then change at any point, if for example there is
a change to the universal constitution of that instance which they do
not agree with and can not resolve with at the moment through debate.

### Migration

Migration allows one instance to select another instance as a parent,
which allows users from the parent instance to migrate their experience
over to the new instance. Since the relationship is only one way,
experience can only flow one way, so while a user is able to bring
experience from an old instance into a new one, they can not then
continue to grow it in the new instance, and bring the greater
experience into the old instance.

## Summary

Knowledge without action does not create value. Action without knowledge
might cause harm. Knowledge with action, deliberately creates value.
Knowledge and action is the responsibility of the individual but
assessing the value of an action is the responsibility of society.
Society must assess an action in order to decide if it provides value.
Some actions might provide value in some subjects and harm in others.
Knowledge, truth and value are not absolute, they are complex and these
systems attempt to formalize the process, and break down these

All humans suffer from the same human conditions that can lead to their

We now have a set of core systems for processing knowledge, information,

# Economy

## Funding and Collaborative Efforts

The previous systems are used in the assessment of value. The following
will cover a proposal for an economy built around this value, which will
enable the perpetuation of value creation, from concept, to funding, to
action, then to the creation of new ideas and value.

### Fund

A fund allows users to pool funds together to fund an effort. A fund
will be a place where funds can be stored, which no individual will have
access to or control over. The only way the funds can be transferred is
through predetermined contracts established by certifications. There are
several different initial types of funds each with different behavior.

E.g If a value of \$100 is reached, the fund will be transferred to x
person. No one can withdraw or transfer funds manually. Funds can only
be deposited.

### Token Voting

Token voting allows anyone to actively participate in a fund's decision
making process. With token voting, when a user contributes to a fund,
they receive equivalent tokens. A token can be used as a vote when
decisions need to be made involving the fund, such as releasing the
funds to the owner for completing a task, or for awarding a winner in a
contest. Depending on the type of fund, decisions can be made with token
voting, experience voting, or a combination of both specified in the
contracts.

E.g A fund is made for the development of an application. A decision
needs to be made about a feature of the application. Users who funded
the development received tokens and are able to vote on the decision
with those tokens. A user that has 10 tokens gets to make 10 votes.

Token voting is different from experiential voting. Tokens represent a
stake in a goal or project, and users can vote with those tokens because
they have this stake. A combination of experience voting and token
voting is possible, established by certification.

### Goal

A goal is very similar to traditional crowdfunding, except that it is
decentralized. The creator of the goal is typically the owner of the
goal. A goal can have a funding objective, which it must achieve for the
owner to begin work. The goal can also have a list of specifications the
owner contractually agrees to complete before or after the goal as
established by certification and its goal parameters. The goal
certification can also use contracts which specify the owner can only
receive the funds through automatic transfer once members or the subject
community votes that it has achieved the goal specifications.

Funding systems can be versatile, and they can behave differently
depending on the certification and contracts they operate on. The
individual starting the goal selects the certification of how it will
operate, and the individuals that are considering to fund the goal
decide which certificates and owners they are comfortable in trusting,
based on the rules/contracts of the certification and the users shared
history.

### Bounty

A bounty is a type of goal, with no owner, which any user is able to win
the bounty fund. Unlike a goal where only the user that started the goal
(owner) is able to achieve the fund. Anyone can start a bounty, and any
member can contribute some value towards the completion of the bounty.
The reward (fund) will be distributed to every user in proportion to the
value contributed towards the completion as assessed by the various
subject communities. The bounty submission is accepted by the community
through vote.

#### Example

A user makes a bounty to make a mass producible solar panel with an
efficiency \> 30%. Users passionate in the subject and objective can
start donating to the bounty. Once the bounty fund reaches an attractive
amount, it will gain the attention of capable individuals. A group makes
a submission with video showing a solar panel, then sends out 10 panels
for individual verification on efficiency to random token holders. The
users post proof that the units received did pass 30%. Everyone votes
and the bounty fund is transferred to the group account who won.

### Projects

Projects are a collection of goals which an owner (individual or group)
creates. When the voters decide that one goal has been achieved, the
funds for the next goal will be unlocked for the project owner.

#### Example

In a project to design an electric scooter the first goal is to create
concept art for the scooter design. It has a funding requirement of
\$5,000. The user then posts proof of goal completion; the scooter
concept art options. The community votes on whether they accept or
reject the submitted proof of completion. If the vote passes, the
\$5,000 fund is released to the user. This allows the user to now begin
work on the next goal, unlocking the funding of each goal as they
progress through the project.

Project backers, not only vote if a goal has been completed, but they
also vote on if they believe the quality is up to their standard. In a
sense this allows for a quality assurance consensus mechanic. A project
can not progress until the backers believe its current progress is good
quality.

The details and behaviors of the contract specified for the goals can be
customized and be selected from a set of common certificates and
corresponding contracts. The general idea is to create a democratic
process for progressing a project and unlocking funds. The details of
how that is achieved is between the project owner and community of
potential funders.

Having a crowdfunded project broken down into several goals that unlock
funding by democratizing the process through certifications,
community/token vote, and factoring in trust into funding progression
solves some of the most common issues with crowdfunding today. It will
no longer be as easy for project owners to fake a project or disappear
with the entire project fund as is the case with the majority of
traditional crowdfunded projects today.

### Trust

Projects can be set up in many different ways. Some options will create
more trust in the project than others, within the community and
investors. One of these options is for the project to become an open
project or owner transferable if it meets failure criteria.

#### Example

A project was funded, but the owner lost interest and disappeared. The
community can vote the project as a failure, or pass a vote of no
confidence. Based on the established certifications, the contracts will
automatically make the project open (open projects are covered in the
next section), or transferred to a new owner based on community vote.
The original owner will automatically relinquish all legal rights by
contract, to anything that was created through the project funding thus
far.

In this example, users are more likely to fund this type of project
setup because they trust that if the original owner fails, the project
will continue.

Owners may gradually increment the funding requirement for each goal, so
that trust grows proportionally with the funding being unlocked, as
opposed to all or a large amount of funding being unlocked initially as
with traditional crowdfunding.

#### Example

Project goal 1 involves some concept art the owner requires only \$100
for. The next goal requires some mock up which the owner requires \$500
for. The 3rd goal requires \$1000 in order to create a prototype. In
this example not only is trust gained incrementally, but the project can
begin before the project is fully funded with all goals, this further
creates trust in that the owner can already have a history of completed
work as the project is in the progress of being fully funded.

There are many more examples of how project trust can be increased.
Projects will have several initial certification options to select from
with pre-set combinations of contracts governing parameters and
behavior. All of this being customizable by the project owner,
accessible and verifiable by the crowd.

### Open Projects

An open project is a community project without an owner. It can also be
thought of as a project with multiple bounties instead of goals, which
anyone can achieve. The community discusses desires for something of
value, whether it\'s a product, service or improvement and then defines
how the project should be executed.

#### Example

1.  A user creates a post about an idea

2.  Discussion is had about the idea and the consensus is that there is
    value to it

3.  A user adds a goal to design it

4.  Another user adds another goal to create a prototype

5.  Many users begin to fund the goals

6.  As the fund from the concept goal grows, users respond to the goals
    with several submissions and one of them gets voted as the winner
    and the goal completes.

7.  The funds are transferred to the creator of the winning concept

8.  The process continues for the other goals in order.

Each project goal is open to anyone to submit content or evidence of
goal completion that the community can then vote on to accept as having
completed the goal. Depending on how the project is set up, users might
win the entire fund from the goal, or it is split amongst all users that
created value for that goal in proportion to the votes they received.

### Other Funds

There can be many other fund types, anyone can create contracts and
certifications to support new fund types such as contests or specific
funds for medical expense coverage that has specific safety checks that
mitigate fraud.

### Decentralized Development

As mentioned before, even if a service or system is decentralized, if
the development team behind it is centralized, that system or service is
dependent on a centralized element in its full function and it is still
vulnerable to exploitation, thus not fully decentralized.

#### Example

A team creates a decentralized project. An organization determines that
they would like to take control of the project, and thus makes a deal
with the team to create backdoor access to the decentralized project,
creating a centralized access point.

For a project to be fully decentralized it must be developed with an
open project process which allows the community to drive its direction
and develop it with no dependency on any central control point.

#### Example

1.  An application specification proposal document is created and set up
    as an open project with dozens of goals and subgoals for each
    specification. After detailed debate and fine tuning the project is
    ready for funding.

2.  Users that see value in the proposed application can begin to create
    scripts, contracts, interfaces, etc for each of the goals specified
    in the open project.

3.  The development community can then test out all the submissions for
    each goal, test for any bugs, and stage in a test environment to
    make sure they are worth accepting.

4.  When the community accepts a goal, the funds appropriated for the
    completion of that goal, are delivered to the users who contributed
    to the completion of that goal, through the assessed value based on
    the votes each contribution earned. This can be through suggestions,
    actual code, mock ups, diagrams, etc, anything the community thinks
    benefited the specific goal in the project.

5.  Once the project is complete, anyone donating to the hashtag of the
    application, for example #openhealth, will receive their portion of
    that donation based on how much value they contributed to it through
    development and beyond.\
    \
    For-profit open projects can have a similar behavior, but any profit
    made is distributed to its contributors accordingly based on the
    voted value contributed by the community. This is covered in more
    detail in for profit ventures documentation.

### Tokens

In traditional crowdfunding, the user has the option of pre-purchasing a
product or service at a discount. The earlier the pre purchase, the more
of a discount received. Projects in open systems also have the option
for this type of behavior with tokens. A token can be purchased at an
established value by the owner, and once the product or service is
ready, they can exchange the token for the product or service.

Another benefit to using tokens is that if local laws permit, the owner
can allow the token to be transferable and thus tradable on exchange
markets. This is essentially like purchasing stock in a company early on
such as in an IPO, in order to make a profit off your speculation of
increase in value.

This will open the door to projects receiving more diverse funding from
investors that are not only interested in the product or service but
want to fund the project to make a profit.

The project owner will be liable for any legal consequences and
responsibilities if tokens are not allowed to be transferable in their
local laws. This type of project is geared towards experienced or expert
project owners. Investors might not have trust in an inexperienced owner
trying to launch this type of project.

## Value Economy

An ecosystem can develop between users that create value and users that
fund those efforts, using the systems outlined earlier.

### Sponsorship

A user can fund a goal, project, but also a subject. When a user funds a
subject they are sponsoring any effort or value created in that subject
by other users. This can be a one time donation or recurring.

Users with experience in those subjects will receive passive income
proportional to the amount of experience they have in those subjects
each time the subject is funded. Funds that are tagged with subjects
such as goals or projects will also receive passive funding from the
sponsorship, in proportion to the amount of value or potential value
they might create as assessed by the crowd in those subjects.

Sponsorship is the driving mechanism of the value economy. Any
individual can choose to sponsor any subject they are passionate about.

#### Example

A user was one of the greatest contributors to preventative cancer
research that helped many. As a result the user had a great deal of
votes increasing their experience in following subjects/communities:
#cancer, #research, #medical. As a result, whenever a donation by other
parties is made to cancer, research, or medical, the user earns passive
income. If a donation is made directly to the research project page,
they earn a much greater share of that donation, since it is divided
amongst fewer users.

### Voting Power vs Passive Income

Voting power is awarded to those who create value as experience. These
experts can now direct decisions on certification policy, projects,
goals, etc, in their respective field with voting power, or they can
maintain a level of experience in order to earn an income through
sponsorship. Any excess funding that an individual does not require, can
be reinvested into communities or subjects that individual is passionate
about, or the user can use their expertise to create a new project which
they can help fund, or find an existing project they would like to fund
or invest in. This mechanic of choosing to use experience as passive
income or voting power creates more scarcity in experience and shows
there is a cost to voting power: only community decisions important to
the user will likely be voted by the user with the cost of experience
and thus income from the user, only projects or ideas the user is
passionate about will be upvoted, and only posts the user strongly
believes are valuable or harmful will be up or down voted.

### Quantitative Value and Purpose

One of the greatest driving forces for all sentience is to make meaning
of existing, to understand purpose, and to arrive at some measure of
success in this pursuit. Currently the traditional measure of success;
money, often does not reflect real value, and we are left in a
meaningless pursuit sometimes referred to as the rat race.

With a value economy another quantitative measure of success will be the
value an individual creates in their respective fields; their
experience. Besides net worth this will be a new figure to drive people
to create more real value in society compared to just net worth alone.

This can result in individuals pursuing their passions and being
rewarded for creating real value in their fields. With the freedom of
being able to join any new field simply by studying the field and then
putting in effort to create some value.

The reward of creating social value will be proportional to the income
received by creating it. This creates more social trust around the idea
of wealth creation compared to today.
