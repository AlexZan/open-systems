We discussed Ethereum\'s transition to Proof of Stake (PoS) and the
concerns raised about potential censorship due to validators following
OFAC recommendations. Ethereum, like other decentralized blockchains,
does not have built-in mechanisms for censorship or compliance with
external regulations. However, individual validators can choose to
exclude transactions associated with blacklisted addresses, effectively
following OFAC recommendations.

This ability to follow external guidelines is not unique to Ethereum but
is a characteristic of any blockchain that allows validators to choose
which transactions to include in their blocks. We discussed how this
applies to Solana as well, another blockchain platform with its unique
consensus mechanism, Proof of History (PoH).

The conclusion is that while validators on Ethereum, Solana, or any
other programmable blockchain can choose to follow OFAC recommendations
or any other external guidelines, this doesn\'t necessarily lead to
network-wide censorship. As long as there are validators willing to
include those transactions, they can still be added to the blockchain,
maintaining the censorship-resistant nature of these networks.
