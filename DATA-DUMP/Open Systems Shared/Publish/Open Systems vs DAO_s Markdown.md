While both a Decentralized Autonomous Organization (DAO) and these Open
Systems aim to empower their communities through decentralization, there
are a few key differences:

1\. \*\*Value Economy\*\*:

\* Open Systems propose a value economy where rewards are based on the
value created and acknowledged by the community.

\* DAOs typically use a token-based model where decisions and power are
based on the number of tokens one holds, which may or may not reflect
the actual value contributed to the system.

2\. \*\*Merit-based Tokenomics\*\*:

\* In the Open Systems, tokens can be earned based on the contribution
of value and merit, not just by buying them. This promotes a more
equitable distribution of tokens based on actual contributions.

3\. \*\*Project-Based Decentralization\*\*:

\* Open Systems emphasize the concept of open projects where anyone can
contribute and earn rewards based on the value they create. This fosters
a dynamic, evolving system where community members are incentivized to
contribute in meaningful ways.

\* DAOs, while they can execute projects, typically have a more static
governance structure where decisions are made based on voting power
associated with token holdings.

4\. \*\*Sponsorship Model\*\*:

\* Open Systems propose a sponsorship model, where users fund a subject
or project they are passionate about, driving a participatory value
economy.

\* In contrast, DAOs are typically funded through the initial sale of
tokens and ongoing revenue generation of the DAO\'s activities.

5\. \*\*Experiential Voting\*\*:

\* Open Systems introduce the concept of \"experience as voting power\"
where users earn voting power through contributions and demonstrating
expertise in a field.

\* In most DAOs, voting power is directly proportional to the number of
governance tokens one holds.
