# Introduction to Open Democracy and Open Systems

This document will attempt to identify areas of global improvement and
provide solutions which will eventually lead to a proposal for a
democratized and decentralized system of collaboration. These systems
have no set use and can be used from small two person organizations to

I begin by identifying problems this system aims to solve.

# Democracy Origins

In the days of tribal hunter gatherers, the tribe was small enough that
anyone could have equal opportunity to voice their opinion when everyone
gathered to make decisions. This could be seen as a primitive form of
direct democracy.

At some point in history, two tribes began to cooperate and if they were
far enough from each other they would naturally select representatives
to travel the distance when decisions needed to be made instead of the
entire tribe going. This could be seen as a primitive form of
representative democracy.

# Representative Democracy

Unlike direct democracy, representative democracy involves individuals
electing other individuals to represent their best interests. There are
several issues with this type of democracy:

## Assumed Best Interest

There is no equal direct communication between the individuals and the
representative and so when the representative votes, they assume the
interest of the general public, and we assume they have our best
interest.

## Promised Changes and Position is Not Guaranteed

Typically we vote on representatives based on the set of proposed
changes and stance they define before they are elected. But there is no
guarantee that the representative needs to follow through with the
promises made.

## Conflict of Interest and Lobbying

A representative can have direct or indirect income sources such as
through businesses beyond their income as a representative, which could
interfere with their duty of making a decision with the population\'s
best intention in mind if it conflicts with a business or personal
interest. A very common and accepted practice that often results in
conflict of interest is lobbying.

## Campaign Costs

Part of the personal interest of the representative is to get reelected
if they are able to, this costs a great deal of time and money and is in
no service to the primary function of the representative but can
interfere with primary duties and might be another point of conflict of
interest.

# Direct Democracy

Unlike a Representative Democracy a Direct Democracy allows each
individual to vote on specific issues, not representatives. While it
solves the issues outlined in the Representative Democracy section, it
faces its own issues.

In 2021 the Swiss voted to increase the minimum wage to \$25 dollars.
While this appeared great to many of the voters, the negative economic
consequences of such a regulatory decision are mainly understood by
economists. But there is no mechanism for the Swiss populus to have an
open discussion with economists about such issues to understand the
consequences.

The problem with Direct Democracy is not everyone has experience or an
understanding in every subject that is being voted on, as a result, they
are inclined to vote on behalf of someone else\'s understanding they
trust to some degree, this creates an incentive to influence as many
individuals and votes.

In 1995 the Quebec referendum in Canada to split Canada vote was lost to
50.58% against the split. Can anyone confidently say that every
individual that voted, comprehensively understood the consequences of
such a vote? Yet the vote that would have had drastic consequences was
marginally denied by .58%. There were many leaders who understood many
of the consequences, and as a result took to the streets and media at
the time to influence votes in their favour. In situations such as this,
direct democracy seems more like an archaic form of representative
democracy without the electoral process, relying on those who are the
greatest influencers.

# Improvements

Instead of being influenced, ideally individuals should be inspired to
understand the issue being voted on. In an ideal scenario, opposing
sides would provide a joint information package that both sides agree
to, outlining the consequences of both options. This information package
would include references to the discussions and debates they had while
arriving at the conclusions for those consequences, with experts
weighing in with their best assessments for the accuracy of those
consequences. With references to support the points being argued. All
voters would then have equal access to all the information, to make a
more informed vote. The concept of an information package is an abstract
representation of a system that will be eventually proposed.

In an even more ideal scenario, a service for the population to have
open discussion on issues with experts, and each other, to crowd source
creative opinions, which would be part of the above information package.
This sort of system, though, would be a double edged sword, in that
crowdsourcing opinions might provide some new and creative ideas, but it
could also create a great deal of discussion messages that do not
provide value, which would have to be filtered or sifted through,
creating a potentially logistical conundrum in doing so. Ideally the
system would organically sift through discussion messages.

There have been several ideal scenarios and systems stated in this
document so far, and there will be more to come. This document attempts
to outline problems, and provide ideal scenarios, finally providing
solutions as systems that should result in the realization of many of
these ideal scenarios solving many of the problems the world currently
faces.

With the above ideal scenarios, where individuals have access to
comprehensive information packages, comes an important question: Just
because an individual has access to information packages, does that
guarantee they will choose to be informed before they vote?

A voter can still choose not to understand the consequences of a vote,
and not vote or instead be influenced by another individual. There are
many reasons for this, but mainly we are emotional beings, it is easier
to feel content with a vote if we believe it is correct, versus
understanding it is correct. Understanding requires time and mental
energy to learn the required information, which might contradict a
position we feel content with requiring even more energy to overcome. Or
it might not be an interesting subject to the voter, but they have a
sense of trust in the influencer and so they vote in their favor.

It seems even with the previous improvements, the influencer incentive
is still there. Maybe the idea then, is to gradually decrease it, by
gradually increasing the amount of individuals that inform themselves.
As stated above, this depends on having the comprehensive information
readily available, but also the motivation to digest the information and
participate in the democratic system to begin with. More on this later.

In an ideal scenario, every voter would comprehensively understand the
consequences of a vote, but this might not be realistic since no one can
force any individual to be informed or understand a vote. The freedom to
choose what we understand is as important as any other freedom. Also an
understanding of a subject is not a binary value, it\'s not that
individuals understand it fully or not, but always some inbetween. The
responsibility to inspire and motivate individuals to participate is
often not a priority of current leadership, it is sometimes not even in
their best interest since influencing is dispensing convenient truths or
lies, but inspiring is sparking the imagination to create and discover
truths.

# Open Democracy

*The principles, systems and concepts I propose here are just that, only
proposals. I expect many will change. As long as this document helps
create principals, systems and concepts which will help sustain the fair
and collaborative process of ever-long change.*

The key proposed difference between previous forms of democracy and open
democracy is that no individual has any inherent initial form of voting
power, but all individuals have equal opportunity to increase voting
power.

Another key difference is that open democracy is a decentralized form of
democracy, which I propose is a form of a democracy that is not
dependent on any form of centralization, as are all previous non
primitive forms of democracy, such as the centralized leger of citizens
and the more modern form of identification and centralized
identification validation for voting. Achieving this has not been
possible until the last decade since guaranteed decentralized trust was
impossible until the advent of blockchain and immutable leger
technology. As such these forums of democracy require these digital
technologies, just like current forms of democracy require ballots.

I propose that an individual\'s voting power can only be increased by
that individual creating value for others. *The use of the word creating
is important, instead of contributing, since it implies something new
must be created, not just contributed as a monetary fund. As it will be
described later, there is no way to financially increase power.*

The value of a creation is determined by all voting individuals.
Individuals vote to assess the value of a creation, and the more value
that a creation was determined to have by voters, the more voting power
the creator achieves.

A creation can be anything that can be documented in an immutable leger,
such as a discussion forum. The creation itself can be a text post, an
image, video, proof of some work or proof of some tangible creation,
etc.

There is no absolute voting power, it is specific to a subject. E.g.
Someone that created value in #technology gets voting power in
#technology. I propose the \# symbol is used to indicate a subject tag
in documentation. Anyone can create value in any subject(s) of their
choosing, and anyone can vote on the value of a creation in the
subject(s) of their choosing.

I propose the value of voting power an individual has should be
expressed as experience. Since they created value in a specific subject
in the past, they now have experience in that subject. Experience is
greater than knowledge alone since knowledge can go unused, while
experience requires both knowledge and motivation; taking action to
apply it in creating value.

Experience is consumed. When an individual votes with their experience,
the experience they voted with is consumed. *This means that an
individual is likely to vote on meaningful things to them, things that
will provide most value in their opinion.*

Downvoting consumes an individual\'s experience to remove experience
from another individual. This can never be performed directly to an
individual, but only through a documented creation that is deemed as
being harmful in the voters opinion. *This results in always providing a
documented reason for the upvote or downvote. Creating an immutable
narrative of the flow of voting power and value.*

An individual has as much voting power as they have experience; an
individual can vote with 10 experience on a creation, or vote 10 times
with 1 experience on a creation, there is no difference. This is a
significant point as it shows there is no importance on identity, since
a user can create as many accounts to vote with as they desire since it
will provide no benefit as they would need to create the equivalent
experience across all accounts or just one. *This concept of identity
having no relevance in function has widespread effects on the system
that will be discussed in detail.*

A post is an entry made in the immutable leger system which I will refer
to as the forum going forward. A post can contain a creation by an
individual or user. An individual can be anyone outside the system,
while a user is an individual that is represented on the system through
an account.

## Example Photography

1.  A user posts a photograph of bird, with subject tag #photography,
    #nature photography

2.  A day later, the post receives 1 vote in both tags. the original
    poster receives 1 experience in #nature, and 1 experience in #nature
    photography

In this example, there was nothing more complex than the artistic
appreciation from one individual to another in the form of giving them
experience in photography tags.

## Example Debate

1.  A user makes a post about why they think a #political policy is bad
    and states their points

2.  Several user attempts to refute their points with their own points

3.  Other users support the original posters post with their own
    responses

4.  Users upvote and downvote the supporting posts and responses they
    think add value to the subject

In this example, there might not be a conclusion, or a right and wrong
side, but there is a clear discussion with consensus from multiple
parties through voting. In the end there is an information package
towards a specific subject that should provide a comprehensive dialogue
with supporting arguments and evidence. So that when it comes time to
make an actionable vote, this discussion can be referenced in the vote,
for all to see (more on actionable votes later).

## Fluid Representation

In this open style of discussion and debating, everyone has equal
opportunity to act in the role of the parliament or the senate. The more
value your discussion point creates in a debate, the more votes it will
receive, the more attention it will gain, and the poster will gain more
voting power through the experience they gain. An individual\'s post or
response might contain the reasoning needed for a vote to pass or not
pass.

A user gaining experience in a specific set of subjects can be said to
make them an expert in that field, and their voting power as a result is
much higher than the average individual. This in a sense can be thought
of as representation from a subject matter expert. At any point, their
negative actions which are deemed harmful to those subjects can result
in losing experience and no longer holding the same amount of voting
power, hence fluid representation.

## Identity-less Solutions

### Voting

Current voting solutions rely on centralized systems that issue, manage
and enforce identity documentation, papers or cards. These systems have
been exploited countless times historically. Since current identity
systems rely on centralized systems, open systems cannot rely on any
traditional identity systems; an open and decentralized system can never
depend on a centralized system, making it indirectly centralized.

Later in this document a method of identity voting will also be
integrated for local and membership voting, though I believe most global
voting will be done through identity-less voting.

### Accounts

With accounts having no identity by default, the focus of open systems
becomes purely creation. With no identity there is no bias associated
with identity, as a result users have an equal opportunity to all
aspects of the system.

It can be argued that the human condition will always contain a plethora
of biases, many of which we are not cognisant of at the time. While
these biases might have had some utility in the physical realm, in the
digital realm, they serve no purpose, and the focus should be on the
value created, not who created it.

Humanity working on improving their biases is out of the scope of this
document, and it is something that should continue to deserve effort.

# Initial Experience

Since the voting system is just a transfer of experience from one
individual onto another for the merits of their creation, experience
must initially be created with the system. I propose 2 methods for
initially creating experience:

A creator can earn experience even if their creation receives no votes,
as long as it did not receive any down votes or reports (more on reports
later). This is the first method for introducing experience into the
system or new subject when no other individual currently has any.

An individual may also earn experience by subscribing as an auditor. An
auditor has no power over any individual, they are simply able to earn
experience by assessing votes(more on this later). To become an auditor
experience is required and must be locked into auditing in specific
subject tags. E.g. When a creation is reported, auditors are selected at
random in order to vote with their locked experience if the creation has
broken any rules, e.g. plagiaristic.

# Actionable Voting & Subscriptions

Until this point we have looked at voting in order to assess value
creation. Voting is also used for decision making. E.g. when a change to
the rules is proposed, or a change to the software that these digital
open systems operate on must be approved democratically.

Actionable voting functions the same as assessment voting except that it
will have a passing condition which executes some predefined action.
These conditions and actions will be defined in smart contract: logic
that is stored in an immutable decentralized leger and executed on the
decentralized system.

Whereas assessment voting has no limit; users can vote as much as they
desire with no end limit of how many votes a creation can receive, an
actionable vote requires a limit in order to achieve a finite point that
defines consensus, in order to make a decision. These can be time based
limits, or vote count based limits. E.g. After some time, if there are
more votes than downvotes, a vote will pass, or if a vote count limit is
passed, the vote is passed. Both of these have their pros and cons and
are suitable for different situations.

Regardless of the voting limit type, actionable votes first require
criteria subjects to be selected. These are the subjects that users must
have experience in to vote if the actionable vote passes. The criteria
is typically defined by assessment vote. All voting criteria that gets
selected must have its own passing vote in order for the actionable vote
to pass. *Think of it as a panel of experts for each relevant subject
that must vote independently of every other subject panel in order for
an actionable vote to pass.* *E.g. if a vote requires both #art and
#tech, then two separate expert groups must both pass independently for
the actionable vote to pass. If one does not pass, the actionable vote
does not pass.*

Subscriptions are a vote count limit based method of voting, it allows
users to subscribe to a post with specific subject experience they lock
in. After subscribing, if an actionable vote is started, only users that
had previously subscribed are able to vote, and the actionable vote can
only pass when there are more than half of the votes to experience that
has been subscribed.

## Example Software Update

A programmer posts an improvement to some software with the tags
#programming and #smart contract. Other users upvote with other specific
subject tags. When the votes begin to settle down, and users have had a
chance to review and comment with no one stating any issues a user then
starts the vote by responding to the post with an actionable vote that
contains a smart contract which takes the software update and makes it
public. Any user is able to start the actionable vote at any time. Since
the post is an actionable vote, only users that have subscribed to the
original post (knowing it would eventually need a vote to integrate) are
able to vote. If there is a total of 1000 experience locked in to
#programming in then the actionable vote requires 501 votes to pass.
Once the #programming vote passes, the #smart contract vote still needs
to pass, as well as any other major tags that were voted on the original
post. Once all the major subject votes pass, the action is executed and
the software update is published.

An actionable vote status is kept hidden or encrypted until the vote
either passes or fails. This is to avoid any groupthink. Users are free
to reply with any concerns or ideas they have at any time.

If a vote fails, another vote can be started at any time, typically
after some concerns that caused it to fail are addressed.

Any experience locked into a subscription can be unlocked at any time,
and fully refunded. Voting with subscription does not consume the vote;
a user can vote on as many actionable votes as they like with the
experience they locked into the subscription. Voting with a subscription
does not earn any extra experience.

# Funding

## Funds

Any user can create a fund. This creates a smart contract with coin
storage in the post with certain rules. There are different type funds
with different smart contract rules, the most common are bounties and
goals.

## Donations

Donations can be made to posts and funds. A donation made to a post,
will send the funds to the original poster of the post. E.g. A
remarkable photo of the grand canyon has received 10 donations totalling
\$200 dollars equivalent in coins.

*I believe the majority of the funds and transactions occurring on the
system will be donation based, and that system will be a majority
donation based economy.*

## Value Economy, Passive Income & Sponsorship

Experience can be used in voting, but it can also be kept in order to
earn passive income in proportion to the amount of experience one has
available. This income is from global tag sponsorship.

A user can sponsor tags globally, locally or somewhere in between. This
means the user is donating over time. E.g. A user that is passionate
about cancer research can sponsor #cancer research, globally in the
system, and every month they can donate 10 coins to the tag. The
donation then gets divided amongst all funds in the system that have the
tag #cancer research in proportion to the amount of votes each fund has.
The funds are also divided amongst all users that have #cancer research
tags in proportion to the amount of experience each user has available
(not locked).

*I believe the entire economy of a system can operate by individuals
creating value, being rewarded with experience and thus passive income,
which they can then choose to redistribute to other funds and creations
they are passionate about.*

## Bounties

A bounty is a fund with the reward being open to anyone that proves they
were first to meet the bounty criteria.

E.g. There is a software problem with a new feature in an open system, a
bounty is created to fix it, and users begin to donate to the bounty
over time. The bounty reaches an amount that becomes attractive to
developers, they now compete to develop and submit the fix as a reply to
the bounty post. When users vote that it has in fact fixed the bug, the
bounty is awarded to the winner.

Bounties can be awarded to a single user or to multiple users in
proportion to the amount of votes. That way if multiple users
contributed to the outcome they will all be rewarded. E.g. 5 scientific
experiments are submitted over a one year period to a study bounty, they
all help prove the same conclusion in different ways and so they are
each compensated in proportion to the value they created in the study.

## Projects

A project is a set of goals. Goals have predetermined criteria that the
owner specifies, beyond just the funding criteria, such as in the
standard goal mentioned earlier.

E.g. In a project to design an electric scooter the first goal is to
create concept art for the scooter design. It has a funding requirement
of \$5,000 crypto equivalent. The user then posts proof of goal
completion; the scooter concept art. The community votes on whether they
accept or reject the submitted proof of completion. If the vote passes,
the \$5,000 crypto fund is released to the user. This allows the user to
now begin work on the next goal, unlocking the funding of each goal as
they progress through the project.

The details and behaviors of the smart contract specified for the
project goals can be customized and be selected from a common set of
smart contracts. The general idea is to create a democratic process for
progressing a project and unlocking funds. The details of how that is
achieved is between the project owner and community.

*Having a crowdfunded project broken down into several goals that unlock
funding through a democratic process solves some of the most common
issues with current crowd funding by democratizing the entire crowd
funding process through the open principles, and factoring in trust into
funding progression.*

### Open Projects

A project is open if no one owns it and all the goals are bounties that
anyone can achieve (more on this later).

### Trust

Projects can be set up in many ways with many options. Some options will
create more trust, some will create less trust. One of these options is
to allow the project to become Open if it meets failure criteria.

E.g. If a project that was funded fully, but the owner loses interest
half way through and becomes inactive, the community can vote the
project as a failed, this will make the project open; a crowd sourced
project. The original owner will automatically relinquish all legal
rights by contract, to anything that was created through the project
funding thus far.

In this example, users are more likely to fund this project because they
trust that if the original owner fails, the project will continue.

One last example is that owners can gradually increment the funding
requirement for each goal, so that trust grows proportionally with the
funds being unlocked, as opposed to all or a large amount of funding
being unlocked initially.

*There are many more examples of how project trust can be increased
(more on this later). I imagine that projects will have several
templates to select from with pre-set combinations of smart contracts
governing parameters and behaviour. All of this being customizable by
the advanced project owner.*

### Tokens

In traditional crowdfunding, the user has the option of pre-purchasing a
product or service at a discount. The earlier the pre purchase, the more
of a discount received. Projects in Open Systems also have the option
for this type of behavior with tokens. A token can be purchased at an
established value by the owner, and once the product or service is
ready, they can exchange tokens for the product or service.

Another benefit to using tokens is that if local laws permit, the owner
can allow the token to be transferable and thus tradable on exchanges.
This would allow speculators to fund projects and earn income off their
investments.

This will open the door to projects receiving much more diverse funding
from investors that are not only interested in the product or service
but want to fund the project to make a profit.

*The project owner will be liable for any legal consequences and
responsibilities if tokens are not allowed to be transferable in their
local laws. This type of project should be reserved for more experienced
or expert project owners. Investors might not have trust in an
inexperienced owner trying to launch this type of project.*

### Token Voting

Token based projects can also introduce the concept of token based
voting. Where each token or x token(s) provides 1 vote in any decision
involving the product you invested in. Token based voting does not
involve any experience, so the user voting does not need any experience,
they just need to prove they are vested in the end product through token
ownership. Token voting can be combined with experience voting to allow
the direction of a project to changed based on those vested in the
project but as well as those with experience in the subjects of the
project. The type of combination can be left to the discretion of the
project owner or community.

# Judicial & Legislative

## Reporting

Any user can report a post, there is no experience required to report a
post but the reporting user must specify what subject the post should
get audited by. The post will then get audited by a random auditor that
has subscribed to the majority set of subjects reported and voted on
that post. Each of those subjects then gets voted on by at least two
auditor per subject, and if any subject has a passing vote, the report
passes. The post then will no longer be public, but can still be
accessed directly for reference. The account of the post will get a
successful report log. If there are no auditors available for any
subject, then as long as there are double the reports to upvotes the
report will be successful. When a report is made, a reason must be
stated.

All decisions can be appealed, for a decision to be appealed it will
require double the amount of votes it needs to pass in order to be
overruled, and so on. E.g. A post that had 10 votes in #art in order to
be successful in reporting, for the reason of plagiarism, now needs 20
votes from at least two auditors in order for it to be appealed.

## Auditing

Any user can become an auditor by locking experience into auditing
specific tags they have experience in. I suggest that any decision
requires auditing to mitigate any exploitation or group think that might
be going unnoticed. Each majority subject of any decision will need at
least two auditors to vote and will require more than half of the
experience voted in that subject on that post in order for the decision
to be successful. E.g. if a bounty has 100 subscribers in #art, then a
submission requires 51 votes from the subscribers, and 51 votes from
auditors in order for it to be accepted.

The auditor\'s responsibility is not to vote on whether they accept a
decision, but to notice if anything is wrong and not pass it if they
think something is wrong. E.g. a project has a goal with an artistic
design that the subscribers are voting on. Since auditors are not
members of the project they have no say in the subjective artistic taste
of its members, they only need to make sure nothing is wrong with the
vote itself.

An individual auditor can only vote as much as half as the votes needed
for the decision or report to pass. E.g. A vote that needs 51 votes to
pass will allow a single auditor to vote up to a maximum of 51/2 or 26.
The next auditor can vote a maximum of 51-26 or 25. If there is a 3rd
auditor selected at random, then they can only vote for the remaining
number of votes up to the original maximum of 26, and so on.

## Rules & Regulations

Each subject can have different rules & regulations that are
democratically created. E.g. #kids cartoons, may not contain rated R
material. When a post is made to a tag, it must abide by the rules or
otherwise be reported. Users may propose rules for any subject as a
post, and have users with experience in those subjects debate, suggest
changes and vote to pass on accepting the rule.

## Claims

The claims process can be used to debate a truth, seek compensation for
damages, or to mount a structured case against a party with claims and
supporting evidence. Any user can make a claim against any party.

A claim is just like a discussion post but with a section that contains
supporting evidence. Each piece of supporting evidence can be voted or
commented on to demonstrate the value it created in the claim.
Commenting on evidence can be used to dispute it or support it. Claims
can link to other claims that support or reject the initial claim.
Multiple claims can be linked together to form a case.

A report is actually just a claim with a resolution being set to make a
post not public.

## Resolution

A resolution is a proposed action to resolve an accepted claim. When a
claim is voted as accepted, the resolution will execute. In the case
that the claim is one party against another party, the resolution can be
set by a single general vote to accept a proposed resolution from either
party. E.g. User A plagiarizes user B's art creation. A claim proves it
and is accepted. A resolution is proposed by User B to transfer all
donations from the original post to them instead. As soon as both users
accept the resolution it is executed. The community can at any time vote
additional resolutions as well. In the above example the community can
vote a resolution to donate the same amount to the #art community. The
vote is based on #art , #plagiarism and #justice. The vote passes and
the resolution is also executed transferring the funds.

## Major Votes

A major vote such as a change to the open constitution (more on this
later) will have certain properties in the actionable vote, such as
maybe one month to maybe a year\'s time in order to accept the vote to
provide plenty of time for debate. This can either be common practice
when creating an actionable vote for major issues, or part of the rules
when creating an actionable vote for #constitution.

Legislative

I suggest that any policy that is voted in must have a full narrative of
its lifetime. From the problems first reported that it tries to solve,
to the debates that shaped it, to the studies that were carried out to
prove it and to any future debates made in order to maintain it, change
it or remove it. By each policy having this maintained and comprehensive
information package, voters can understand the entire lifetime, need and
oppositions of the policy.

## Local Jurisdiction

Open Democracy is not a replacement for local government; the actions of
individuals whether they are from a post discussed on an open forum, or
a bounty or project, an individual\'s actions still must comply with all
local policy and laws. Following local policy and laws are completely
the responsibility of the individual performing the action. What might
be permitted in a local jurisdiction and encouraged by a discussion
post, might not be permitted in another local jurisdiction.

# Open Member Democracy

Local and private organizations where each member gets an equal vote may
have an initial vote to enable an open member democracy system. This
will allow only users who are members to vote within an open democracy
system; the decision is first discussed in the forum, the subjects are
identified with tags, and the majority subjects are selected as the
criteria for voting. Now any member with experience in those tags can
vote as usual, but someone that is not a member has no right to vote
even if they have experience.

This will be helpful for membership organizations, such as, a company
with owners that need to direct the company, or a town or village that
has a local community who need to make local decisions. This system will
help membership organizations transition into an open democratic system.

The discussions of the decisions unless private are recommended to be
had publicly, that way reason and creative input can be globally crowd
sourced, even if the final decision is made by the private members.

# Open Collaboration

Until this point creations mostly as an individual effort have been
described, such as a creator posting their work or ideas to a forum. I
also propose systems that help with collaboration creations, such as,
open posts and open projects.

## Collaborative Creation & Licences

All content that has a license specified can be reused by other content
creators. E.g. Ff someone posts a nice musical beat to #music
production, and specifies an income share of 15% in their licence,
another user could use that beat to create a song with, and any income

## Open Post

An open post is similar to today\'s wiki concept, where users contribute
content to a subject, but in this case the proposed content is voted in
by users who have experience in the subject. I propose the system for

## Open Project

An open project is similar to a project, but all of the goals are open
to anyone to complete. I suggest that any creation made for the project
and accepted as a bounty winner will still provide a profit share to the
creator, but the share amount will be modified democratically, not set
by the creator as with personal creations. This would allow all creators
that contributed to a project to share the profit of the project as it
has success. The duration of the term and amount of the profit share can
never be modified by the creator in this case and it will always be a
democratic decision.

I suggest that goals can be split into sub goals, recursively, so that a
complex goals can be broken down into multiple sub goals with multiple
creators (described later)

E.g A user wants to collaboratively create an open source electric car.
The idea is roughly discussed, people come up with various versions and
features they would like. A goal is created with the concept of the car,
then another for a 3d rendering, another for the mechanical design and
so on. Each goal begins to gain funding either through donating to the
project directly or to the individual goals. The funds grow and users
begin to respond with submissions. They are voted on and winners
selected. Once the open source car design is completed, another local
manufacturing project can begin and receive funding to start locally
producing them, repeating the process but for producing and delivering
the vehicles.

# Open Principals

Thus far the document has described several concepts and systems all
based on similar principals. This section details these principals.

## Open

No one can own a system that is open. There can be founders, creators,
maintainers, contributors, but no individual, group or company can have
any legal right to ownership of the system.

*Individuals may earn profit share from their creation, and can own
their creation, but no one can own an open system that the creation
exists in. Though a system or service can be conceived, funded and built
through open systems, it does not have to be open itself and so
individuals or companies can create a system/service and still own it,
but it will not be designated open and thus can not be fully integrated
with other open systems.*

## Open Democracy

Equal Opportunity to Voting Power: No individual, group or company can
have any power over any other individual in an open system. Any action
in an open system must be performed democratically. No one can be
designated with any type of power over others. Everyone must have the
same opportunity for creation and thus voting power at all times.

## Decentralized

An open system can not be shut down or modified without community vote.
It can not be controlled by any individual, group or company. An open
system also can not have any dependency on a centralized system.

*Being dependent on a centralized system means that an individual, group
or company can choose to shut down the system that it is being dependent
on, and thus the dependent system will be non functional; The dependent
system is indirectly centralized by the centralized system. No one can
have authority or control over any part of a system.*

## Transparent

All data is transparent. Any data going into an open system, any data
modified or deleted from an open system is logged and viewable by all,
publically on an immutable leger.

*A critical point to understand is transparency does not mean lack of
privacy. All individual data is anonymous. Therefore private data can
never be leaked unless someone gains a hold of a specific private key
(\~password), which could be a doctor you give permission to for
example, or if someone gains access to where you store your private key.
It is easy to secure a private key, just like securing your keys to your
home.*

*A consequence of all data from everything being open to all but yet
anonymous, is that the data can be studied by anyone in order to drive
science, make discoveries, predicting events, etc. The data however can
never be used for covert or exploitive purposes, since it is available
to all and owned by none.*

Encrypted data is also permitted as long as it follows all other
principles. If data is encrypted, it must be encrypted for all, with no
one having the ability to decrypt the data. Data can only be
encrypted/decrypted by consensus or by a consensus contract. Data is
only mutable through consensus.

## HealthCare

### Global Health Record

A standard that is maintained through open democracy. It will create a
means for any organization or individual to gain access to a secure
record by being given an access token from the individual or an
authorized user.

### Diagnostic

An open and global service for diagnostics. Patients are able to report
symptoms and an AI or human diagnosticians can guide the patient or
authorized user through collecting the data needed to rule out causes as
a diagnosis is approached.

As the diagnostic AI studies more sample data from all patients in the
global health record, it will become more accurate and be able to notice
corroborative nuances from patient data that doctors might miss or
require a team several weeks to determine.

### Preventative

Smart devices and consumer health measuring devices are becoming
commonplace. AI can monitor health through the data collected from these
devices. Along with genetic scanning, AI can keep a close eye on any
genetic predispositions to potential health threats to mitigate or stop
them before they develop. By looking at various corroborating patterns
emerging from various devices and data sources on the patient, AI can
flag certain conditions for priority diagnosis to rule out any imminent
risks or danger.

### Emergency

Peer to Peer emergency response system. Using various devices, users can
signal that they are having an emergency. E.g. if a heart rate monitor
no longer gets a reading, it will prompt the user if they are fine, if
there is no response, the device will signal the emergency network of a
potential emergency. The system will select the best emergency
volunteers that have previously been selected to respond to emergencies
with specified emergency training, based on distance and qualifications.
An audible alarm on the device will sound to notify responders to the
area. The profile to the individual will be sent to the selected
responders with a description of what potentially occurred and a
suggested course of action. After locating the individual the responders
can carry out instructions while local emergency services are on their
way, having also been notified. In the case of specialised equipment
being needed in a timely manner, drone equipment may be dispatched.

### Holistic Care

By connecting with devices and applications which track our nutrition
and exercise, AI can also make suggestions on improving health. E.g.
When and what type of exercise to perform completely custom tailored to
the individual. What food to eat based off predicted nutritional
deficiencies, or genetic specificities.

### Community Test Centers

Community funded test centers can be established in order to measure
specific data required by doctors or predictive AI. e.g. A blood lab in
a developing area can be globally funded or locally funded. Devices in
test centers or clinics can also be funded individually.

### Anonymous Data

I suggest data can be available from all global health records
anonymously, which can be a great source of training data for AI and
scientific advancement studies.

## Science

Experimental design templates developed and rated by the community which
may then be used to run experiments by the scientific community. Tests
may be performed and documented, reviewed, collaborated and
corroborated.

This process works perfectly with open systems as scientific members can
gain experience and thus funding in their specific fields. Tests can be
funded as bounties and studies can be funded as projects. E.g. If a
discussion in the open forum reaches a point that requires more
conclusive data, a bounty could be started to provide scientific
evidence.

## Education

Educational content creators will be able to earn passive income by
having their lesson or course through sponsorship. The more value the
educational content creates, the more experience and passive income the
content creator earns. Direct donation and sponsorship on the content
itself is also possible. If the content is used in a commercial work,
the set revenue share % can also be observed as with most other
creations.

### Test First

I propose a lesson should never be assumed to be required, but a test
should accompany each lesson and If the student passes the test, the
lesson is not given, if the student fails the test, a simpler test is
given, and so on, until the student is able to pass a test, at which
point the last failed test\'s lesson is given followed by the next
failed test and so on.

For this system to function, all teaching materials must be broken down
into unit lessons that try to teach or focus on a single teaching point;
each of these unit teaching points can have multiple tests.

I propose a system for referencing unit lessons in already existing
digital educational content. Where tests can be linked to these unit
lessons through the proposed system. E.g. a video timecode that covers a
unit lesson, or a paragraph that covers another unit lesson can be
linked to several test questions.

#### Testing

Results from tests can be shared on an account in order to display a
user\'s knowledge level in various subjects. This is different from
experience which is creating value with knowledge/skill. This might be
useful when being considered for a job or a project.

#### Retention

Doing a subject refresher sometime after the initial study can easily be
done through test first, quickly zeroing in unit lessons forgotten,
while reinforcing the passing tests.

## Connectivity

A Peer to Peer network system where users can create network points such
as wif or other protocols such as 5g, and serve data at a rate of their
choosing. Users can then register to the network with their devices and
use the service at the declared rate. In areas that do not have open
network coverage, users can continue to use traditional network services
and switch back and forth such as with using dual sim in mobile devices.
Users in areas with no open service can either become a provider
themselves as a business opportunity, or put in a request. Users can see
the total request amounts in areas and easily decide if it is worth the
investment to create a network access point. Users can start as simply
as creating or joining a wifi hotspot on the open network.

The protocol of the open network will be designed and maintained through
an open democratic process.

## Production and Manufacturing

Traditional consumerism involves a company trying to predict what
consumers want, creating and selling it. With open projects, users can
discuss what they want, fund the things they really want, and have
manufactures bid for manufacturing open designs. This is in a way
reverse to the current process. There are countless benefits to this
process, there is much less overhead cost with marketing and design, and
manufacturing is much easier to localize and as a result it is more
agile. Manufacturers must maintain good ratings in order to have their
manufacturing projects funded and make sales.

Open designs can constantly improve as consumers try out the latest
prototypes or products, then provide reviews or feedback on the open
forum. Forks in designs as new open projects can naturally emerge as new
segments are identified.

## Energy & Waste

An open protocol for community energy and waste services will allow
users to put in requests for different services. This will allow
communities to create a bounty for local energy and waste projects such
as a community microgrid or compost program, or it will allow
individuals or groups to create energy services for the community.

The open energy service will allow users to see areas that have a high
demand for services, and those that have existing adequate services.

Business oriented users can provide services such as recycling even if
it wasn't an in demand service, but can provide funds or credit to users
for providing recycled material.

Various services can be designed and provided through open
manufacturing, such as; biogas digester, solar and wind microgrids,

### Service Stations

Users can register to become a service station provider on a service
station map. This will allow them to provide a public electric charging
station, or hydrogen station provider at a rate of their choosing. Users
can also put a request for a station on the map also, providing
potential profit data to potential service providers.

E.g. a user can place a charging station at the front of their garage,
and allow cars to park and charge for a specific cost per kw.

## Transportation

Open rideshare services can allow users to post their vehicle and going
rate. When a user needs a ride or transportation service, they can post
what they need, and in real time, service providers can bid on providing
the service. The providers will remain anonymous but the user will be
able to see their rating and location and can decide who they would like
to go with.

This can also be used for the transportation of goods in the same way,
and for long trips a logistics AI can create a relay or series of
transport providers to connect them together for the most efficient way
possible.

The protocol of the open rideshare service will be designed and
maintained through an open democratic process with localized changes to
adapt to different governments and environments.

## Service Inheritance

A service can act as a parent for another service. E.g. A barter service
that facilitates the trading of belongings can be used as a base service
to build a more specialised service for trading home grown vegetables.
When an improvement is made to a parent system, it can then be passed
down to all services that use the parent. That way there is a reduction
in duplicate effort and code.

# Integration with Existing Government

## Initial Phase

Once the core systems are operational. I suggest local political members
can choose to run for an "open" platform and party. The policies will be
custom tailored by the party to match the locality, but the discussion
of all policies will be held on the open forum, which will be a global

forward, anyone can propose their own policies. But policy is only
accepted by representative vote through traditional representative
government. The open systems will not be used to vote in policy in the
initial phase, only to create an open dialogue between policy makers and
everyone else; to crowdsource policy debate.

A log of all policy decisions can be kept in the immutable decentralized
leger and referenced any discussions in the open forum that had effect
on the decision. This way policy management and creation will be more
transparent than with traditional representative government.

##  Open Democracy Non Critical Phase

In this phase the open system should be used by the majority of the
populus. This can be tracked by allowing users to link their traditional
id to their account on a voluntary basis. At the point in which the
majority of the population has done this, a vote can be put forth in
order to allow non critical votes to be decided through open democracy.
The vote will be done traditionally, such as with a referendum. If the
vote passes then decisions deemed non critical by the representative
government will be made through an open democracy vote. It is still up
to the representatives to execute the majority of the actions from the
decisions.

## Global Policy

As adaptation for open systems increases and global policy evolves, it
will be easier for any states using open systems to interact with each
other, since the global policy for such interaction is constantly
evolving by all the users on the system.

There will ofcourse be limits, such as if a city level government is
majority open, but a provincial or state level or federal level
government is not open, it might limit the open global policies that the
local government can act on. The open government must then wait for the
local non open government to have the open party grow in popularity
until it becomes a majority, or they can propose a separation referendum
to become a global open state.
