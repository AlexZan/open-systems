Just because the regulation is created and enforced does not mean all
individuals will like or want to follow the regulation. Therefore
regulation should be optional in order to meet specific certification,
not a general regulation, when possible.

For example, instead of creating a general forum regulation that no one
is to post nudity, offensive words or other offensive material, instead,
an individual can choose to only view posts that have been certified to
be generally offensive free. This like every other definition is defined
through a democratic process. There can be many more specific
certifications to custom tailor what a user might find offensive,
without forcing it on everyone else. Users can choose to see all posts
in their raw form, or they can choose a set of certifications they think
will protect them. When a user posts, it is their responsibility to
choose the correct certifications to make sure they reach the right
audience, e.g. child friendly. If they select the wrong certifications
there will be automated consequences for being successfully reported.

Eventually there can be automatic AI certifications that the user can
run which would grant the correct certifications for the specific
content they are posting. This does not guarantee perfect certification,
but it would provide a great ever improving firewall between the user
and the community. The responsibility should also not fall on the user
if they are using AI certifications but on the quality of the ever
improving AI.

This will provide the individual the choice to experience something more
or less regulated without forcing the experience on anyone else,
allowing everyone to make their own choice.
