Claims is the system and process for conflict resolution. A user must
provide evidence to support their claim. There will be multiple
competing resolutions. There will be multiple optional competing
theories to explain, dissect and understand the conflict.

This process can be used beyond just the immediate resolution process
but to help understand and avoid future conflicts; this is where
proposed theories are valuable; they try to make sense of the conflict;
they try to find patterns in similar conflicts.

#### Questions

Verdict is deciding if a claim is true or not. Resolution is the
proposed action taken to resolve the conflict. Should these have
seperate votes, or should voting on a resolution automatically vote the
verdict as true. If separate, which should be voted first?

By voting the verdict through voting on a resolution, it might add
ambiguity. Some might think they are just voting on a preferred
resolution, when in this setup, they would be voting on a resolution and
a verdict. In that case they should be separate. Maybe resolutions
should be optional. Sometimes a situation might call for just fact
checking through a claim process, such as a dispute between historical
events, where parties provide their own supporting evidence, and a
verdict is made.

#### Verdict in a flux

If a verdict is time based, then new evidence after the time of a
verdict might change the verdict, and this could keep oscillating
indefinitely in theory, in some cases. That means that new resolutions
might be reproposed.

# Cases

### A company posts a fake ad (asks for money to be e-transferred to have an item shipped over) on a 3rd party marketplace.

1.  A user makes a claim with the evidence as

    a.  A screenshot from the email

    b.  A voice recording of a call asking for money upfront

2.  A few other users submit their own similar evidence

3.  The evidence and claim begins to get votes in #: fraud,
    investigation, shopping fraud, justice

4.  Users begin to post resolution proposals.

5.  A resolution gets votes to more than half of the claim subscribers,
    the resolution passes.

    a.  The resolution states that an entry into a list of known scams
        is added with the details and evidence.

The value in the above; the 3rd party marketplace does not use open
principals, and brand new fake accounts can post fraud ads. There is no
way to prevent this behaviour with 3rd party systems. Although by
tracing the occurrence and pattern, it can provide pressure to have that
3rd party system, in this case the market place make security
improvements, or provide data to the consumer to avoid that 3rd party
system, or what to watch out for in that system.
