## When will the vote go through? What are the conditions?

When more than half of all the global experience is voted to pass the
vote. But this is not a consensus. Consensus would be going through, if
it is not blocked. But if the condition is not votes, it must be time
based. Or it could be read based, which is essentially a free vote,
saying, yes you have read it, and did not down vote it. What is the
value of this method over voting? It saves experience. Everyone gets a
chance to impact the vote by marking it as read, even if they dont have
experience. But then once again, couldn't someone make 1 million
accounts, and hit read 1 million times. (this should have no impact),
because anyone can block it. So read does not actually make the vote
pass, it just defines a sense of everyone having acknowledged the
proposal. So making bot accounts and marking a bad proposal as read will
have no impact, since it can never pass quicker than a day anyways, so
in one day someone will have a chance to block the bad proposal. If the
vote is time sensitive, then a global vote can make it pass quicker than
24 hours if everyone votes quickly to make it pass with less
deliberation (the deliberation time will be between the post time and
the vote pass time).

## What about someone refusing to remove their block after the community has achieved consensus?

The community can then begin to downvote the block post. If the posts
reach +50% of the global experience, the block will be outvoted
(canceled).

## How will blocking engage deliberation?

A block post must provide a detailed reason for blocking the decision.

you are open to deliberation!

Tldr: fluid representatives. Just by earning experience you are a
representative in the subject you gained experience in.

Since identification is not encouraged, the selection will be purely on
public anonymous stats, which I assume would mainly be experience.
