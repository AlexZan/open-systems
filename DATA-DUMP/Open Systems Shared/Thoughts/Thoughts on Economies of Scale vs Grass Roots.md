Both Economies of Scale and Grass Roots have their purpose, but
identifying their purpose will prove valuable.

A small community trying to produce steel will be nearly impossible to
make profitable, while a national or global company producing steel can
be profitable.

On the other hand, a national company producing food, has reason not to
care (stakeholder interest is only profit) about the nutritional quality
of food, other than making the food appear great. Consumers have no
objective way to determine the nutritional quality of a food and so
there is no feedback loop to maintain high quality food.

The idea is that a society will benefit off economies of scale only when
there is a feedback loop between consumers and producers as to the
quality of the product being produced. In this case of steel, this is
very simple to test and objectively, and so a feedback loop between the
steel producer and the steel consumer.

Therefore, in the case of food production, until there is an objective
and consistently; systematic method of testing nutritional quality by
the consumer, food through grass roots production, such as in homesteads
or small farmer communities, will be more likely to provide the
nutritional content needed for healthy sustenance, while at the cost of
losing economies of scale. Once a system is developed for a quality
feedback loop for food production, then economies of scale can once
again produce high quality food.
