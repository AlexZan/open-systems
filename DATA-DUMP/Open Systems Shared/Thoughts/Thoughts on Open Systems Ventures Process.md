# Output

What will the venture produce with each iteration? Is it repeatable, is
it different each iteration. An iteration might be product based e.g.
coffee mugs, or an iteration can be time based e.g. software development
for 1 week.

# Input

All the different items that contribute value to each iteration.

# Initialization

What are the initial steps required before iterations can begin. Items
in the initialization can be bid on by the venture members with
competing offers. E.g. Tasks include researching competition, creating a
website, and making a promotional video. Member A bids 6 hours, Member B
bids 5 hours. Since Members B bid is more competitive, they will be
responsible and will be compensated 5 hours \* established rate. The
rate must have been established before bidding started. If a rate of 60
dollars was set, then 5 \* 5 or \$250 would be the compensation for the
first task. The compensation would then be repaid back from the debt
profit share, usually 10%. So if an iteration brings in \$100 profit,
then 10% of \$100 is \$10. In 25 iterations the debt would be paid back
for the initial task compensation for the first item.

# Liabilities

1.  Property

2.  Personel

3.  Production

## Property

If equipment, land or building is at risk of damage, deposits must be
established in order to cover the cost of repair by each member using
the property.

## Personnel

If the work poses risk on the member, training and/or insurance must be
developed

# Production

Deposits should be made by members with the suggested full value of a
production item in the case that a production is compromised by a
member(s).

# Dissolution

Stating what happens with any assets that have value. Typically If the
assets have value, they can be bid on by members, or sold off by members
with an agreed upon commission share in the sale price, or sold by an
agreed upon broker.
