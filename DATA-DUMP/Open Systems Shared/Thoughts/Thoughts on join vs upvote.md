If no post belongs to any individual, then upvoting and downvoting might
not make so much sense, because;

1.  You either are interested in a post, in which case you join it

2.  You don't agree with something so you suggest a change or make a
    reply expressing your disagreement.

3.  You report it because it is breaking rules

4.  Or you ignore it because it does not interest you

What is the point of downvoting? Why would someone downvote? What is the
point of upvoting if you can show your support by joining it.

Then we will show how many members a post has. These members are those
that will vote on a post. These members are those that will make
suggestions and corrections.

Can this be recursive? If the traditional thought was that each post can
be its own post then can you join a reply?

Use case: if a reply is a simple "no", and everyone agrees with it,
traditionally they would upvote it. In this case, joining a reply of
"no" makes no sense. What are you joining? You should be able to express
that you agree with this. In which case upvoting still makes sense.

Or there could be reactions, such as laugh, thumbs up, thumbs down, etc.
and the default sort could be based off thumbs up, which really is just
a replacement for upvote and downvote.

In this case it makes sense to keep upvoting and downvoting for
responses, since if you joined an original post, you need to interact
with responses. If you dont agree with a reponse you downvote it, and
vice versa. If you dont agree with a suggestion you downvote it, etc.

What about something that is funny but has now affect on the original
post. Then you can react to it with a list of emotions.

What if you are interseted in an original post, but dont know if you
agree with it. Does join mean you are in support of it? It sounds that
way. Maybe subscribe is a better way to interact with a post.

Maybe before you can upvote or downvote or interact in any way with a
post or any of its reponses/suggestions, you must be subscribed to it.
And by interact with the post in anyway you automatically subscribe to
it.

So then the only purpose to subscribing is that you receive
notifications of any interactions with the post, and you are notified to
vote on them.

Maybe there can be an option to commit to a post. When you do this, you
must vote on the post within a timely manner, by committing you earn
more karma per interactions.

If you subscribe to a post, you do not earn any extra karma like
commiting.

# Conclusion

Upvoting and downvoting do have utility for both the original post and
the interactions/responses. But there is also utility in having a
subscribe and a commit which are features that can come after the
prototype.
