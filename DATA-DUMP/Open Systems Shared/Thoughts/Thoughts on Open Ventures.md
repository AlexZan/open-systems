A venture is a combination between a brand and a direction. A brand has
specific rules that establish an expected quality, as well as the
reputation of its members which provides user trust. A direction
determines what the venture will attempt to develop into.

An open venture should be able to operate without any need for open
systems. It is based on several principals.

1.  No time based/hourly compensation/pay

2.  No human resource management, members will manage themselves

3.  Member liability; a member must have a deposit upfront greater or
    equal to the total consumer cost of the task they are performing.
    E.g. if they are building an item worth 50 dollars, they must have
    50 dollars deposited into a common account

Thoughts on share offers

If two people (A,B) start a venture, and break down two tasks, build
item, sell item:

Cases:

1.  A offers 80% share to build, B is left with 20%, and accepts

2.  A offers 95% share to build, B is left with 5%, and refuses

    a.  At this point the project venture can end, they can reoffer, or
        A can choose to build and sell.

3.  A offers 50% share to build, B is left with 50% and accepts. A few
    projects later, A reoffers 80% since he thinks his work is
    contributing more value than the current 50%, B accepts 20% and they
    continue.

4.  A new builder trainee C joins the venture, and two new tasks are
    voted in by everyone, build training, and trainee build. Since the
    two new tasks only contribute to the initial build task, they fall
    under it. A offers 80% for build training, and C accepts the 20% of
    the trainee build. Since the new tasks are under the build tasks,
    build training is 80% of build which is 80% of the total project,
    which is 80% of 80% or 64%, while trainee build is 20% of 80% or
    16%.

5.  Once C has enough skill to require less training and do much more of
    the work, while A does much less supervision and work, C offers 80%
    and A accepts 20%. A can now focus more on his own build project
    where he takes 100% of build, with little supervision over C's
    project for the 20%.

6.  A new builder trainee D joins the venture, C offers 60% for build
    training and A offers 80%. A and B concerned that C is not ready to
    offer training since he is still new, propose a rule that a (non
    trainee) builder must have 5 products sold with no negative feedback
    in order to provide feedback, in order to be a trainer. The vote on
    the rule passes and as a result A trains D, until C has a few more
    successful iterations, then takes over training for A.

What governs the price? Supply and Demand. Therefore it is much better
to use shares for split than absolute values whenever possible, since
the price of the finished thing will not be reflective of the absolute
values during production.

Who governs venture rules? Who gets to vote on passing a rule? Whoever
owns the brand. If a brand is expected to produce a certain quality, it
must have rules to support that quality. These are established by
whoever maintains the brand.

Can a brand be open? If a brand is just a set of policies, then yes.

Would the above example be open? No because it is a venture the two
initially started. They are the founders of the brand. But it could be
open, in which case all rules could be voted in by anyone with
experience. If it is not open, then just the founding members would need
to vote on rules. Would new members such as C and D be able to vote?
Maybe they will somehow earn community/belonging points in the brand the
more they participate, this + their experience will allow them to have
voting influence. Also in this specific case if A and B want to retain
the right to decide who joins the venture, it must remain a private
venture. If it was an open venture then anyone could essentially become
a member and begin building, selling through the brand.

Thought : Difference between corporation and government?

A government provides rules about how it operates that you have no
choice in. A corporation (in a healthy market, not a monopoly) provides
rules about how it operates that you have a choice in, you can choose to
use them, or another competitor. In a monopoly or cartel situation, you
no longer have that freedom of choice. A government is intended to put
society\'s needs first, and thus it is intended to create rules about
how it operates that put society\'s needs first. A corporation puts its
profit first, and thus creates rules that best create profit, but since
they are competing with other corporations, those that treat society
best perform best in the long run. In the 21st century, the difference
between corporations and government is minimal, and in some places they
are one in the same.

# Assets

What happens with assets that are needed for the venture. E.g. a URL,
website codebase, app codebase.

A url could be purchased by one of the members, but this might add
complications if the member decides to leave. Therefore the member
should not own the asset the venture depends on. In which case what
happens to the assets when a venture dissolves. If the assets have
value, they can be bid on by members, or sold off by members with an
agreed upon commission share in the sale price, or sold by an agreed
upon broker.

# Deadlines

Tasks which other tasks depend on should have established deadlines.
E.g. a sale is made with a promised delivery of 1 week, shipping will be
2 days, therefore the items should be manufactured in 4 days. If the
member misses the deadline, then the user is compensated with their
deposit

# Ownership

Who owns the venture? No one. The initiative to start the venture though
can be rewarded with a founder share. In a non democratized venture, the
brand is owned by the founder, and the rules are established by the
founder.
