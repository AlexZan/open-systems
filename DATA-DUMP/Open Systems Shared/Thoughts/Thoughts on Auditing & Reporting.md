## Initial Experience vs Audit before post

The idea that posts do not get their base/initial experience a day after
posting, and instead must not be blocked by a consensus group in order
to award the initial experience. Since they both result in a user
potentially assessing the post, whether they are in a consensus group or
just looking at new posts, it results in very similar behavior. The main
difference is that consensus groups can have a penalty for not
participating, therefore increasing the odds that a post will exploit or
break rules.

Downside to this is there would be more upfront functionality required.
I think it is better to have basic experience functioning without the
need for auditing. But at the same time, without being able to report,
people could just exploit experience with no consequence, and I would
say this is not actually functioning. Therefore reporting is still
required, and thus auditing is required.

A huge benefit to audit before post, is that the poster does not have to
pay to post to blockchain, the auditor would pay to submit the post, the
report, and to assign the experience to the poster and to themselves
immediately. ~~It can be the choice between the auditor/group or the
poster to claim their experience first.~~ The auditor would be
submitting their report, and they would be the ones paying, which would
execute the post/report/experience process. This would reduce the
blockchain write by half. This is a huge win in favor of audit before
post. The biggest benefit being that posters no longer have to pay for
posting, **making posting free was the goal of this system and this
solves it.**

It might be argued that, the downside to audit before posting is that,
not everyone is given equal opportunity to assess the post since it

## Recursive Reporting/Blocking

What is expected when a report is blocked, but then that block is
reported.

Case:

1.  A post is made

2.  Post is reported as fraud

3.  Report blocked

4.  The block is reported as insubstantial

One of these two reports is false as they can both not be true when they
are mutually exclusive. If the first report is false, then the consensus
group of the report will block it, e.g. insubstantial (providing no
evidence). If this block is not itself blocked, then it will pass, and
the first block will be removed automatically.

But it could be argued that the post itself is a claim that it is
authentic, and the counter is the first block. Therefore one of these
two claims must be false, and further blocking is unconstructive. If the
first block is deemed false (by the consensus group), it will be
removed; this will involve blocking the initial block, taking it back to
the previous example.

What if the second block is false?

5.  A post is made

6.  Post is reported as fraud

7.  Report blocked

8.  The block is reported as insubstantial

9.  The block to the block is reported as insubstantial.

The deeper into the recursion the more confusing it becomes. What if
after the 2nd block, it must pass with a vote. This would mean that
deliberation is no longer an option. This is not ideal, since all
options should be deliberated. Also a vote is always an option. At any
time consensus or a consensus group can be overridden by vote of the
masses.
