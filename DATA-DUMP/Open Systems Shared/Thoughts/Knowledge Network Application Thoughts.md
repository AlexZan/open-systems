## Subjective Truth

Just because a truth is subjective, does not mean it can not, or should
not be proven wrong. If one justifies the interference of someone\'s
freedom with their truth, they are required to prove their truth.

If truth is subjective, then two people can have two different truths
about the same reality. Everyone has the freedom to choose what they
believe in. But when it comes time to communicate about that reality,
they need to reach a common truth, an objective truth between them in
order to be able to interact correctly. To interact with another, we
must have the same understanding of reality. When one parties actions
affect another\'s freedom, and that party tries to justify their action,
they are then and only then required to prove their truth.

Since reality can not be directly observed, then there can only be
comparisons of subjective truths. If one party that holds a truth has no
suffering/conflict from their truth, then there is no benefit to
competing or comparing their truth against another, and another has no
right to do so. If one party is causing suffering to another party
(including a member of their own) because of their own truth, then the
party affected by the truth has every right to contest their truth,
since they will make the case that their truth is affecting their
freedoms.

## Peaceful vs Harmful belief

If a belief is affecting the freedom of others, it can be argued
harmful. If a belief does not affect the freedom of others, it can be
argued to be a peaceful belief.

## Truth vs Knowledge

Knowledge is the set of truths that are closest to approaching reality.
Knowledge attempts to be objective, whereas truth can remain subjective.
Knowledge is what is achieved after the conflict or competition of
truths.

One can have knowledge on a belief of god, but one can not have
knowledge on god. One can study the writings of a belief, such as
christianity, and have knowledge on those beliefs, but they can not
claim they have knowledge on god. I can read about a theory about aliens
invading next Thursday, and I will have knowledge about the theory, but
I can have no knowledge on the invasion itself, since there is no
evidence to support the belief, and it proves by all means, false. One
can have knowledge that a false thing was said, but one can not have
knowledge about a false thing.

One can prove someone else that their belief is false, but they have no
requirement to change their peaceful belief, and one has no right to
force a peaceful belief change, but knowledge attempts to be universal
and thus everyone has a right to debate false knowledge.

"If it works, dont fix it" is the truth. The pursuit of truth is
approaching reality as close as needed for peace. Knowledge is what
comes out of the conflict between truths. Knowledge is our best attempt
at truths that apply to everyone, objectively.

## Freedom vs Belief

Just because someone believes something, they are not required to
validate it, unless they choose to do so. No one can force them to do
so, this would be in violation of their freedom. Only when their belief
infringes on another's freedom are they required to validate it.

E.g. if someone borrowed 1 dollar from another, but does not believe
they did, this does not change the fact that they owe a dollar.

One must justify their actions to prove their innocence, if their
actions are based on their belief, then one must prove their belief to
prove their innocence.

One must prove another\'s actions unjust to prove their guilt, if their
actions are based on their belief, then one must disprove their belief
to prove their guilt.

Is a belief only false if tested? No, a belief is a belief if the bearer
of the belief believes it matches reality. They do not have to test it
or prove it. It is up to the receiver of the belief to determine its
truth if they choose to do so. A contract/certification can be used to
enforce the testing of beliefs for different interactions.
