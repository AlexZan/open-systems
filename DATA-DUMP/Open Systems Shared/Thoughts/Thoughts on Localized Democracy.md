The ability to vote within a localized time-space, based on a forum of
membership or range of effect. This document will try to consider what
is right to vote and what is based on.

Cases:

A makerspace tool purchase

1.  It can be decided that each member gets one vote for being a member,
    or,

2.  Members vote with their accumulated experience, or,

3.  Members can start tool goals, and the first one to get funding gets
    purchased.

A park getting shut down for a parking lot

1.  The vote weight can be dependent on distance from the location.
    Since those closer to the park are more likely to be dependent on
    it.

    a.  How is location verified?

    b.  How is the duration which you have been at a location get
        verified, and how does duration factor in?

Someone might be passing through, staying only for a short time, should
their vote influence the decision. Does someone that own land versus
renting for the same duration in the same location have an effect on
location? By owning land it can be argued that they have a vested
interest in the area, but that is not guaranteed, the only guarantee is
that they are more likely to have a vested interest in the return on
their investment. Unless the land was not an investment, and was passed
down. This seems to be about identifying a "care" value for the
surrounding area through the possession of land, when instead, there are
actual actions of care for the area. The same concept of voting for
experience, but instead of a non localized community, a virtual
community, it would be a localized community. The idea then is a p2p
system for ranking value contributed to the community. What about then
someone that has contributed to a developing community from halfway
across the world, should they have a greater weight in deciding on a
park? It may be argued that the park does not affect them.

There now seems to be two ideas of weight over decision making, a
quantifiable value of value contributed to the local community, and the
value of impact by a decision through distance/time value.

From this I derive a fundamental concept; parties that care about a
localized outcome should not necessarily influence it beyond suggestion,
as the parties that are affected by the localized outcome must have the
responsibility to learn from the outcome of their own decision.

E.g the community suggested that the outcome of the coffee mug claim was
not in the buyers best interest, but the buyer ignored it and might have
had a learning lesson as a result.

In this case the affected weight is simply a function of space-time, or
distance duration. And so location and duration needs to be proven.

This can be done with incentivising check-ins. This might conflict with
maintaining privacy and anonymity. Maybe anonymous accounts can check
in, and build weight in that area over time, while not revealing who
they are in real life.

Can be done between neighbours, friends, and businesses with incentives.

Maybe users can gain points in neighbourhoods and establishments by
checking in. for businesses these can be rewarded for loyalty, for
neighbourhoods this can be coupled with experience to creating voting
power,

## Membership

A user can join a group and as a result is able to cast a traditional
vote on matters of membership.

## Check Ins

1.  Users within range of each other get asked at random if they would
    like to check in with each other to earn some neighbourhood points.

2.  When a user selects yes, it will share their name and their picture
    (optional)

3.  If both select yes, then they are given 1 day in order to check in
    with each other.

4.  To check in they just need scan qr or nft to digitally handshake

## Establishment Check Ins

1.  Establishments can have static check in scanners or the owners use
    their smart devices to scan check ins.

## Privacy

Since checkins tie an account to a location, the accuracy of that
location check in can be preset to a range. E.g. accuracy within 20km,
accuracy within 5km, accurate, etc.
