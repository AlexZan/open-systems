# Initial Vote to Open Local Democracy

An organization where each member gets an equal vote may have an initial
vote to enable an open local democracy system. This will allow only
users who are local to vote on issues deemed local. The rest of the
democratic process process for local voting is similar to global voting.
The decision is first discussed in the forum, the subjects are
identified with tags, and the majority subjects are selected as the
criteria for voting. Now any local with experience in those tags can
vote as usual, but someone that is not a local has no right to vote even
if they have experience.

This will allow local organizations that are not decentralized to
transition into an open democratic system while still maintaining their
central function. The transition from there on out into a fully open and
decentralized organization can gradually happen.

### What about in developing areas with only a few households in entire communities having internet and a device?

There must be a way to share internet/device. In this case there will
need to be some rolls filled by the community. Such as a notifier that
lets users know they have been given a notification.

When they are given the device, they can login with their code. Since

### What about in an area with no internet and only one device for one community?

Someone will have to routinely take the device to a connection area,
then bring it back to the community. Notifications for each user can
then be processed the same as the previous example. Any outgoing data
will then be queued, and the next time the device gets data, it will
deliver the data. The individual users do not have to authenticate while
online, they can sign their outgoing data offline and it will deliver
when the device is online.
