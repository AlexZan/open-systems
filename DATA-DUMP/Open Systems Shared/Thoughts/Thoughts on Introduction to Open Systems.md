# Network over Hierarchy

Preferring a network order, of many to many versus preferring a
hierarchical order, with one to many.

#### A commerce start-up example

The Open Systems concepts will be explored through a narrative: The
MugShop Startup. This story will cover most of what could go wrong
traditionally and each solution using open systems.

###### Quick Disclaimer: Though a commercial startup example is used, Open systems is not a system exclusively for startups. Comercial Startups is just one of many paths used to understand Open Systems. You can think of open systems as a large cave system with many entrances. To grasp its entirety many paths must be explored, from different directions. This is just one of those paths.

###### 

Two people want to start a commercial venture. They each have enough
combined experience to create the venture. For simplicity\'s sake let\'s
sake the venture is creating artisan coffee mugs and selling them.

One person, Aye, is skilled with sales and moderate with clay
manufacturing, the other person, Bee, is skilled with clay manufacturing
and has all the tools and shop to produce them.

Traditionally, they might choose to split the profit 50:50 in an even
partnership, even though their contribution might not exactly work out
to be 50:50. As time goes on, one of the partners might start to slack,
forcing the two to conflict over fair compensation. Eventually the
company might dissolve because of lack of cooperation. The partner who
did not slack decides to restart the venture on their own as the boss.
They hire some employees and now begin to pay them hourly, but once
again, the employees can still slack and still get a constant pay rate.
The boss now must also be a manager and be overseeing they are producing
at the expected rate.

These are just a tiny fraction of the many challenges a traditional
business might face. Now lets look at the Open Systems approach to
solving the above problems, but which also eventually open the door to
many more solutions.

Since this is an open system venture, it must favour network over
hierarchy. Meaning there ideally can not be a person with any sort of
power, control or privilege to value or property over another person.
Since there is no "boss" or owner, then no one can be responsible for
things like wages, rate of production, or quality control.. Different
approaches must be taken to achieve a commercial venture using open
systems, and as we will see the advantages will far outweigh the
downsides.

Traditionally an owner or boss hires a manager that oversees operations,
sets wages, monitors employees are utilizing their time which they are
being compensated for.

In this case they can be both thought of as owners, or neither, the
concept of ownership is not used. Instead the two founders; Aye and Bee,
begin by creating a value contract for the product they wish to produce;
the coffee mug. This contract outlines the breakdown of every item that
provides value towards the finished product or service.

For example:

1.  Shop & Tools : 10%

2.  Materials : \$1 per mug

3.  Creating the base clay mug : 25%

4.  Glazing the mug: 30%

5.  Hardening the mug with heat : 10%

6.  Testing the mug: 5%

7.  Selling the mug: 20%

Together they create the list of items and their corosponding values in
the contract, and when they are both in agreement, they sign the
contract.

### Success

For their first mug, the skilled person; Aye did all the skilled labour,
items 2 - 4. The seller; Bee made the sale; item 6 and received payment,
and provided A with their share. If the item sold for \$20 then \$15
went to Aye and \$4(+1 materials): \$5 went to Bee.

Even A gets in on some of the sale action and makes some sales to
friends, family, coworkers and even a few local sales through online
classified ads (since both Aye and Bee live in non competing areas). In
these cases Aye gets 100% of the sale, all \$20 dollars.

*A value contract is a pre-established agreed value of each item that
provides value towards the finished product or service.*

### Quality

One of the cups had a quality issue; the glaze job is not as good
quality as it looks in the pictures. The buyer shows pictures
demonstrating the decfects. Because it was Aye's job to skillfully
complete the glaze. Aye is solely responsible for any damages as per the
contract. They offer to return part of the money for a discount, a
return, or an exchange. Aye covers the cost of the decision.

*In an Commercial Open System, In order to handle liability for work
quality that depends on skilled labour, the full financial
responsibility falls on the skilled labourers.*

### Trainee

Things are going great and demand is growing. Bee, who has been doing
sales, decides to start contributing to actual mug making, but his skill
is not established yet. Open Ventures that disclose the member roles are
more likely to build confidence.

If consumers see an inexperienced clay manufacturer listed, this might
also hurt their confidence. A solution is to use a Quality Assurance
Contract. This contract establishes tests for every item in the value
contract. The contract also specifies that whoever is signing their name
on a test is responsible for half the liability of that item.

E.g. if an item was defective, the tester who signed off on it is liable
to cover half the damages, the skilled labourer covers the other half.

Both Aye and Bee sign the contract. Bee begins to make his own mugs,
many fail, and he must cover the material cost of each failure.

*The more open a commercial venture, the more consumer confidence the
venture will have.*

### Damaged Equipment

Bee was using a tool in Aye's shop and damaged it. Aye creates a
contract listing the value of the worst case scenario damage value for
each tool that will be used. Both Aye and Bee sign it. In some cases
this might be the full cost of the tool. As a result Bee chooses to only
use some less expensive tools until he gains more experience.

*Asset liability contract helps protect equipment against damage.*

### Growth

They are both making good sales, but they would like to quit their day
jobs and make their main source of income, and so they begin to focus on
growth. After reviewing open system options further, they decide that
incorporating a broker would increase customer trust.

A broker is responsible for executing several contracts. Instead of one
of the venture members being paid directly by a customer, the payment
now goes to a broker. The broker inturn notifies the members that
payment has been received. Payment is now held for a pre-established
period of time (In this case lets say a month). In the case of a
dispute, the payment can be returned from the broker. The broker will
also mediate disputes. These responsibilities can be outlined in various
contracts such as a payment contract and dispute contract.

The value contract must be redone in order to include a brokerage % in
it. The broker and members resign the contract.

*A broker is a 3d party (non conflict of interest) service that can
execute payment and dispute contracts/protocol.*

### A New Member

With the broker, they are now able to offload much of the customer
service responsibility, as well as gain more consumer confidence in
their new policy, contracts and broker. As a result they see a large
boost in demand.

They create a job posting for new members. Several candidates are
interviewed. They hire an experienced clay maker, and a less experienced
maker but who they thought had a great passion for it.

The new members sign the value contract, as well as the asset liability
contracts for the tools they will be using. The experienced member signs
the liability contract against all the tools, and begins working on
creating their own inventory of mugs to sell.

### Order first vs Product first

Switch to order first since team is growing. But product first will
allow for immediate shipping.. Order first will allow custom designs.

### Vacation

Leave when they want, work when they want, they are their own boss
choosing how much to earn.

## Thoughts

Open systems can be thought of as a bag of things; policies, protocols,
contracts, etc. You can pick and choose which to use. Using the right
combination of these things will create more consumer trust.
