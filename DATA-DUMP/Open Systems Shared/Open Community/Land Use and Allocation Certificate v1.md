## **Land Use and Allocation Certificate v1.0**

### **Purpose**

To define fair and transparent rules for land use, allocation, and
personal rights within the community. This certificate respects the
initial landowner's legal authority while protecting all members'
autonomy, personal space, and access to shared resources.

### **Section 1: Ownership Context**

**1.1 Foundational Ownership**

- The land on which this community is established is legally owned by a
  founding member.

- Legal rights remain with the landowner in all interactions with
  external law.

**1.2 Expressive Commitment**

- The landowner commits to honoring the principles of expressive
  politics within the community.

- Land use decisions within the community will follow expressed
  agreements rather than unilateral control.

### **Section 2: Personal Allocation**

**2.1 Allocation Rights**

- Every full-time member is entitled to a personal plot for housing and
  minimal private use.

- Plot sizes are defined based on available space and community
  agreement.

**2.2 Customization**

- Members may build, grow, or modify within their plot, so long as it
  does not create harm or violate shared agreements.

- Long-term use implies stewardship, not legal ownership.

**2.3 Revocation Protections**

- A member's right to their plot cannot be revoked without a clear
  violation of community agreements and a cohesion-based resolution.

### **Section 3: Common Space**

**3.1 Shared Responsibility**

- All common areas (gardens, paths, kitchens, etc.) are maintained
  cooperatively.

- Use is non-exclusive and must respect posted or expressed norms.

**3.2 Modification Protocols**

- Any major changes to common space must be proposed and reach at least
  80% cohesion among active members.

**3.3 Preservation Clause**

- Natural areas designated as shared or preserved may not be altered
  without deliberate community process.

### **Section 4: Founder Authority & Limits**

**4.1 Reserved Powers**

- The landowner retains legal and financial liability rights (taxes,
  permits, etc.).

- They may intervene to prevent legal conflict with external
  authorities.

**4.2 Expressive Checkpoints**

- In all internal matters, the founder agrees to follow expressive field
  logic and community norms.

- Major decisions impacting others require either community cohesion or
  certificate-based agreements.

**4.3 Abuse Protection**

- If any member (including the founder) violates agreed norms, the group
  may initiate formal mediation or propose adjustments to this
  certificate.

### **Section 5: Updates & Exit Options**

**5.1 Versioning**

- Any updates to this certificate must be versioned.

- Existing members must manually express support for the updated
  version.

**5.2 Exit Agreement**

- If the land is ever sold or transferred, a community dialogue must be
  initiated to renegotiate terms.

- Members will have a 30-day grace period to decide whether to stay,
  adapt, or relocate.

### **Summary**

This certificate balances the legal realities of individual land
ownership with the expressive autonomy of a decentralized community. It
protects members' space, outlines shared responsibility, and creates
checks against unilateral power while ensuring legal compliance.
