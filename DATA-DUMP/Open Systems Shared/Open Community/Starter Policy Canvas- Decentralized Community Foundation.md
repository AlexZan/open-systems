## **Starter Policy Canvas: Decentralized Community Foundation**

### **Context**

This document defines the initial policy expressions, governance
structure, and community protocols for a decentralized community of
10--20 founding members. All expressions will operate within the legal
limits of the local and national governments. No internal rule or
certificate may override external law.

### **Section 1: Foundational Principles**

**1.1 Alignment with Law**

- All actions and policies must comply with local municipal,
  provincial/state, and national laws.

**1.2 Expressive Autonomy**

- Members may express preferences on community policies via expressive
  field protocols.

- Expression fields will guide, not enforce, behavior unless cohesion
  thresholds are met.

**1.3 Certificate-Based Participation**

- Community-wide decisions and norms will be scaffolded using Policy
  Certificates.

- All members may opt into certificates and propose exceptions.

### **Section 2: Initial Policy Certificates**

**2.1 Good Neighbor Certificate**

- Respect shared spaces

- Noise curfew at 10pm unless otherwise agreed

- Participation in monthly maintenance rotation

**2.2 Eco-Responsibility Certificate**

- Minimize single-use plastics

- Composting required unless opting out with alternative system

- Solar-first energy preference

**2.3 Shared Infrastructure Protocol**

- Use of common areas is first-come, first-serve unless reserved

- Members co-fund maintenance via monthly contributions

- Building designs should prioritize passive insulation and resource
  efficiency

**2.4 Living Autonomy Certificate**

- Right to customize your home within visual harmony agreements

- Indoor activity autonomy (unless causing external harm)

- Gardening and off-grid systems are permitted

### **Section 3: Conflict Resolution**

**3.1 Mediation First**

- All disagreements will go through a mediation process defined by the
  group

- Mediators may be selected from within the group or externally

**3.2 Escalation & Cohesion Review**

- If no resolution is reached, the system will analyze cohesion data and
  flag majority-aligned norms

- Conflict flagged as a policy for community discussion

### **Section 4: Membership & Decision Process**

**4.1 New Member Onboarding**

- New members are introduced to the active policy certificates

- They may express support or exceptions upon arrival

**4.2 Policy Changes & Proposals**

- Members may propose new expressions at any time

- Policies become standardized once reaching 80% cohesion among full
  members

**4.3 Enforcement**

- No coercive enforcement is allowed unless a policy has passed the
  cohesion lock threshold and is legal under external law

- Violations are handled through dialogue and consensus tools

### **Section 5: Tools & Systems**

**5.1 Expression Dashboard**

- Digital or paper-based system to express values and track cohesion

**5.2 Certificate Management System**

- Members can browse, opt into, or fork certificates

- Popular exceptions are logged and reviewed quarterly

**5.3 Logging and Transparency**

- All changes to policy, certificates, and exceptions are timestamped
  and reviewable by members

### **Summary**

This starter policy framework lays the foundation for a peaceful,
expressive, and legally compliant decentralized community. It emphasizes
shared responsibility, environmental stewardship, expressive governance,
and consent-based coordination. It is expected to evolve through
community-driven expression and adaptation.
