## **Land Co-Ownership Certificate v1.0**

### **Purpose**

To define the structure, responsibilities, and expressive governance of
land held jointly through a legal entity (e.g., trust, cooperative, or
corporation). This certificate enables proportional co-ownership,
protects individual and shared rights, and ensures transparent
decision-making within the legal framework.

### **Section 1: Ownership Structure**

**1.1 Legal Entity Designation**

- Land is held under a legal entity (e.g., a cooperative, non-profit
  corp, or community trust).

- All contributing members are registered as beneficiaries,
  shareholders, or legal participants depending on jurisdiction.

**1.2 Proportional Ownership**

- Ownership shares are assigned based on documented contributions
  (financial, labor, or resource-based).

- Shares entitle holders to proportional influence in land-related
  matters, but not unilateral control.

**1.3 Trust Responsibility**

- The entity acts as custodian---not exploiter---of the land.

- It must act in accordance with foundational certificates and
  expressive community norms.

### **Section 2: Land Use Rights**

**2.1 Private Plot Allocation**

- Each co-owner is entitled to a personal living or working plot.

- Plot sizes are allocated in proportion to ownership unless otherwise
  agreed.

**2.2 Autonomy of Use**

- Owners have full creative and functional autonomy within their plot,
  provided it does not harm others or violate core agreements.

**2.3 Common Area Access**

- Shared spaces are open to all co-owners and managed collaboratively.

- Use requires adherence to shared etiquette and maintenance
  responsibilities.

### **Section 3: Decision-Making Protocols**

**3.1 Expressive Governance First**

- All decisions affecting land use, development, or resale must follow
  expressive politics principles.

- Expressed preferences are recorded, and cohesion is calculated to
  determine alignment.

**3.2 Major Action Thresholds**

- For high-impact decisions (e.g., selling land, major construction),
  both:

  - 80% cohesion across members

  - AND a legal vote/consent mechanism aligned with the entity's bylaws

**3.3 Certificate Versioning**

- This certificate, and any policies built on top of it, must follow
  manual version approval.

- Updated versions require explicit re-approval from each co-owner.

### **Section 4: Financial Contributions & Liabilities**

**4.1 Shared Costs**

- Taxes, infrastructure costs, and repairs are shared proportionally
  based on ownership shares unless waived or redistributed through
  community expression.

**4.2 Contribution Adjustments**

- Members may increase their ownership percentage through additional
  contributions.

- These must be transparent and agreed upon via both expressive and
  legal channels.

**4.3 Exit Clauses**

- Members may transfer or sell their share according to bylaws and local
  legal constraints.

- A right-of-first-offer should be granted to existing members.

### **Section 5: Legal Compliance**

**5.1 Supremacy of Law**

- No policy or expression may violate applicable property, zoning, or
  corporate law.

**5.2 Interface with Government**

- The legal entity acts as the liaison for permits, taxes, and
  compliance.

- It must operate transparently and provide access to all governance and
  financial records.

### **Summary**

This certificate outlines how land can be shared through a legally
recognized entity while maintaining expressive politics as the internal
governance method. It provides the legal stability of formal ownership
with the flexibility, fairness, and adaptability of decentralized
consensus.
