## **Core Expression Protocol (CEP) v1.0**

### **Purpose**

To define the foundational logic for how expression fields behave,
interact, and stabilize within the Expressive Politics system. This
certificate establishes universal rules for calculating cohesion,
managing policy types, resolving conflicts, and ensuring global ethical
coherence.

### **Section 1: Policy Classification**

**1.1 Policy Types:**

- **Gradient Policies**: Expressed on a continuous scale. Averaged
  regionally. (e.g. pollution levels, tax rates)

- **Binary Policies**: Yes/No expressions. Require cohesion lock to
  activate. (e.g. murder legal, slavery legal)

- **Threshold Policies**: Latent policies activated when local cohesion
  passes a specific threshold. (e.g. emergency response, universal basic
  income)

- **Private Preferences**: Non-coercive, non-governing values. No
  cohesion required.

### **Section 2: Field Mechanics**

**2.1 Expression Vectors:** Each citizen's policy preferences are
registered as multidimensional vectors.

**2.2 Cohesion Calculation:**

- Cohesion = 1 - Standard Deviation of Expressions in Region

- Thresholds:

  - \< 0.5: Disagreement Zone

  - 0.5--0.7: Active Debate Zone

  - 0.7: Emerging Consensus

  - 0.9: Stabilized Policy

**2.3 Boundary Emergence:**

- Boundaries form when adjacent zones have a cohesion difference \> 0.4

- Soft interfaces become hard if binary conflicts emerge

### **Section 3: Global Ethical Resistance (GER)**

**3.1 Global Cohesion Weight (GCW):** Continuously calculated for all
major policy dimensions.

**3.2 Cohesion-to-Resistance Ratio (CRR):\**
CRR = (Local Cohesion × Population Size) / Global Cohesion

**3.3 Activation Thresholds:**

- CRR \< 0.01: Policy remains inert

- CRR 0.01--0.1: Triggers global alert and review

- CRR \> 0.1: Policy enters probationary activation (monitored)

**3.4 Red Zones:** Divergent regions with policies violating high-GCW
norms are marked as Red Zones. These may be isolated, flagged, or
subject to intervention.

**3.5 Core Ethical Anchors (examples):**

- Murder = Illegal

- Slavery = Illegal

- Bodily Autonomy = Guaranteed

- Consent = Required for all binding agreements

### **Section 4: Certificate Inheritance & Updates**

**4.1 Certificates may inherit CEP logic** as their foundation.\
**4.2 Exceptions to CEP mechanics must be flagged and explained.\**
**4.3 Changes to CEP require \> 90% global cohesion** across all current
adopters.\
**4.4 Certificate updates must reflect a higher version number.\**
**4.5 Users on an older version must manually express support for the
updated version; updates are never automatic.**

### **Section 5: Enforcement Interfaces**

**5.1 Policy Statuses:**

- Proposed

- Active (stabilized)

- Inert (blocked by CEP)

- Contested

**5.2 System Responses:**

- Inert policies are non-binding

- Contested policies trigger negotiation

- Red Zones trigger transparency alerts or ethical interventions

### **Summary**

The Core Expression Protocol provides the ruleset upon which expressive
governance is built. It ensures coherence, protects global ethics, and
enables scalable expression and governance through data-defined
thresholds and consent-based logic.
