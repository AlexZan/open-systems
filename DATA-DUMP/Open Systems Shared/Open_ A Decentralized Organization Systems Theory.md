# The Knowledge Graph

The knowledge graph can be thought of as similar to a neural network of
ideas, growing through the stressors of criticism; through the rigorous
empirical and scientific process.

A new idea, would link to another old idea node, which either supports
or disproves the new idea. The end result is an idea with supporting and
disproving ideas linked to it, creating a supported weight value.

For example, if there is a new study that proposes a new hypothesis.
Advocates of competing ideas would then link the ideas they support to
the new idea with a negative bias. Initially the new idea would have
high negative support, but as it gains exposure and review, the
competing idea might lose advocates that are now supporters of the new
idea. There might be new data and studies that support the new idea that
can be linked to increase its support. At one point it will go from
negative to positive support, while the competing ideas will continue to
fall. At a later point, it might become the most supported idea with a
small lead. Once the lead grows beyond a threshold set by consensus, it
becomes the accepted theory and maybe gets referred to as a law.

If there are two or more competing ideas, generally the one currently
with more support, through supporting ideas and data, is just that, the
more currently supported idea. Support is a weight calculated by the
recurcursive net support of every linked node down the chain. Competing
ideas that are close to each other are considered to be in ongoing
debate. While competing ideas which have a clear winner in support mark
the idea that has been reasoned to be the current reality. This of
course can always change, at any time, there is no absolute truth, just
approaching the idea of truth through the creation and critique of
ideas.

This outlined operation requires some form of formal consensus to

## Certification Over Censorship

I propose that instead of censoring ideas or people (canceling them), we
transition into certifications. These certifications are openly
developed by the open community. Anyone can propose a new certification,
or fork an existing certification. Any individual can subscribe to any
certification, and this will filter out content based on the
certifications automated filtering rules. As well, when an individual
posts an idea to a network, they must explicitly select the
certifications they believe it meets.

This results in networks without any censorship, where users can post
the most harmful things imaginable, but also the most creative and
helpful critique that might otherwise get censored. It is a double edged
sword which certification tries to mitigate the edge facing creativity
and criticism. A user with many certificates enabled will have much of
what they considered harmful filtered out.

E.g. A children\'s content community will have many certifications that
will automatically filter out any harmful content for children, as well
as the posters must explicitly select and abide by all the top childrens
content certifications in order to reach a wide target audience.

The control of content is between the poster and the viewer, not the
responsibility of a centralized authority. Viewers can choose how safe
they wish to be, and posters can choose how wide of a target audience
they wish to achieve.

This brings up the subject of deception again. If a poster is deceptive
or maybe just ignorant of the certification they are explicitly
promising to abide by. This is handled and discussed in a later chapter
on decentralized judicial systems.

## Open Certificate

An open certificate is a set of rules to filter out data or define
expected behavior. An automated function (either human coded or AI
driven), will take in data, and filter it out some of that data. A set
of human interpretable rules will set the expectation for behavior from
a program or entity that has that certification. Certifications are
managed in a marketplace of competing certifications.

## Certification Loop

The knowledge network that will maintain these open certifications will
also abide by them. A certification for following an established
scientific process for example. The same individuals and organizations
that use these certifications will be the same entities that propose
improvements to them. This will create a lifecycle feedback loop that is
open to all.

\*\* The community that is maintained by certifications is also the
community that proposes ever growing improvements to certifications,
forking and updating existing certifications.

## Decision Tree

with other open systems.*

## Open Democracy

Equal Opportunity to Voting Power: No individual, group or company can
have any power over any other individual in an open system. Any action
in an open system must be performed democratically. No one can be
designated with any type of power over others. Everyone must have the
same opportunity for creation and thus voting power at all times.

## Decentralized

An open system can not be shut down or modified without community vote.
It can not be controlled by any individual, group or company. An open
system also can not have any dependency on a centralized system.

*Being dependent on a centralized system means that an individual, group
or company can choose to shut down the system that it is being dependent
on, and thus the dependent system will be non functional; The dependent
system is indirectly centralized by the centralized system. No one can
have authority or control over any part of a system.*

## Transparent

All data is transparent. Any data going into an open system, any data
modified or deleted from an open system is logged and viewable by all,
publically on an immutable leger.

*A critical point to understand is transparency does not mean lack of
privacy. All individual data is anonymous. Therefore private data can
never be leaked unless someone gains a hold of a specific private key
(\~password), which could be a doctor you give permission to for
example, or if someone gains access to where you store your private key.
It is easy to secure a private key, just like securing your keys to your
home.*

*A consequence of all data from everything being open to all but yet
anonymous, is that the data can be studied by anyone in order to drive
science, make discoveries, predicting events, etc. The data however can
never be used for covert or exploitive purposes, since it is available
to all and owned by none.*

Encrypted data is also permitted as long as it follows all other
principles. If data is encrypted, it must be encrypted for all, with no
one having the ability to decrypt the data. Data can only be
encrypted/decrypted by consensus or by a consensus contract. Data is
only mutable through consensus.
