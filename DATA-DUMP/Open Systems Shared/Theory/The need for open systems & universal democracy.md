I will attempt to analyse these issues, and propose what they all have
in common. I will eventually propose that they are all due to issues of
closed systems, from this we will begin to define open systems, which
will eventually lead us to the concept of universal democracy.

I will not describe what the issues are as much as I will attempt to
explain my thoughts on their causes.

#### Global warming

Scientific data supporting global warming and its effects is being
suppressed or misrepresented by those in power whose financial and
position of power interests would be threatened by anti climate change
actions.

#### Human Rights and Access to Justice

In some parts of the world, information is being suppressed on human
rights violations. Some parts simply do not have a mechanism for voicing
their concerns, or if they do, there is no infrastructure to assess and
respond to those concerns; there is no effective justice system. In some
parts of the world that do have a justice system, there is often
unaddressed and unstudied systemic bias in the justice system workforce,
whether it is in policing or the judicial system.

#### Pollution and Waste

Similar to global warming, corporate interests compete against any
actions which might alleviate pollution and waste. The current system
incentivises waste by making it the more economical solution

#### Economic disparity/poverty/homelessness

A healthy capitalistic system is not a zero sum game; wealth should be
created by both parties after a trade. What makes a capitalistic system
unhealthy is the exploitation of systems put in place to enforce fair
trade. E.g. if a person lies about a trade, but they are not held
accountable, the trade is not healthy, and it is lose-win instead of
win-win.
