# Preface

Open World's initial simplicity is intentional, through that simplicity,
complex properties and behaviours with vast social impacts emerge.
Initially it is a simple platform for community funding and creating
ideas.

I have been developing this concept for over 15 years; it is my life\'s
work. I have been adding to the concept each year, but it was not until
recently that I learned about blockchain technology which is needed to
create the concept into a reality, starting with a functioning
prototype.

# An Idea for Change and Improvement

The concept starts with an app that will have an open discussion forum
similar to that of Reddit. Users can post ideas, concerns, needs, etc.

What do you want to improve? What change do you want to see? What are
you willing to do to make it happen?

A person or a community can simply post about a problem they are facing
or about a product/ service they would like, or they can protest a
current harmful policy. These are just a few of the many examples that
the forum can be used for.

Someone can post about wanting more research on a given topic (e.g.
cancer) or suggest some solutions such as early detection of cancer. The
possibilities are endless, anyone can create a post/cause about anything
that will ultimately lead to a positive change that benefits society. If
other people agree with the post and want to offer support, they can
contribute to the cause.

There are several ways to contribute; providing philosophical insight
that people agree with, making a logical statement, providing vetted
research data that has been achieved, creating a design, manufacturing a
prototype, etc., are all examples of contributing to a cause.

Another way to join is to donate to the cause and its bounty. A bounty
is the sum of the donated currencies that have been funded. The bounty
is held securely in a blockchain.

## UpVoting and DownVoting

If you believe in a cause, idea or solution (a post) you can upvote it
or add a bounty, if you disagree with a post you can downvote it.

Upvoting is different than adding a bounty. Users initially only get one
vote per post, but can donate to the bounty as much as they like. If a
post gets too many downvotes, it will not be as visible, even if it has
a large bounty. An example of this would be an unethical cause that
someone is willing to fund, which most people disagree with. Posts that
go against policy can get reported and removed automatically. People can
upvote or downvote a post or a contribution to the post. If it is an
excellent post or contribution, it will achieve many upvotes and will
begin to move towards the top of the discussion forum to gain more
visibility.

In summary, to contribute to a cause, people can create the initial
post/cause, contribute content to a post, donate coins to the bounty or
upvote. All of these actions are interconnected and help bring good
ideas to light and drive positive changes forward.

Once a cause is achieved, the bounty will then be rewarded to all the
users who contributed based off community vote.

### Let's look at a quick example:

A traffic light does not seem to change half the time. You pull up to a
red, and want to make a left, but it often skips your left advance. You
are left waiting 5 or more minutes for it to change. You create a post
describing the problem. And create a goal to fix the broken advance. You
fund the goals bounty 3 dollars worth of coins. You can also make a
print out, "Let\'s fix this left advance" with a qr code that links to
the post and tape it to the intersection like a garage sale sign. You
can also tag the post with a geo location so users can find it easier.

After a few weeks, the bounty has grown to \$1500 and 6000 people have
upvoted it. Someone replies that they will be filing the paperwork to
fix the problem, and posts pictures showing the filled out paperwork,
and eventually of a receipt that it was submitted. A few weeks later
someone notices the advanced light is working and votes to award the
bounty for the above response. They also comment saying "seems fixed
now". Someone else comments to the reply saying "i saw workers there
this morning, looks like it was fixed". A few days later 3000 out of the
6000 initial post voters also vote to award the bounty. The award vote
passes and the bounty is transferred in full to the user that submitted
the paperwork. There is a holding period on the transfer for any
disputes.

## OW Principals

### Doctrine

OW will have apps, decentralized systems and services, but OW is not an
app, a system or a service. Another way to think of it is, the community
will make apps, services and systems that follow the OW Policy and
principles which will naturally work together.

### Decentralization and Ownership

There is zero centralized decision making, all decisions are based on
community vote or smart contract (pre-programmed and automated) based on
existing policy (which also was previously community voted). There is no
centralized control point to alter or shutdown the system. There is no
governing body, no individuals that hold any power, no corporations in
charge, no stakeholders. No one owns OW. No one has the responsibility
of deciding for others. Everyone is responsible for themselves and
society as a whole, but nothing in between; Full Decentralization.

Any service within the system can not have ownership. There can be
founders of the service and/or concept for due credit, but no one will
have any special or extra privilege, control, rights or entitlement to
the service.

### Direct Democracy

OW is about voting on ideas and solutions, not voting on people or
political representatives.

### Profit

OW itself cannot profit; It is not an entity in anyway to profit, but a
doctrine. People and groups can only profit from their contributions to
causes. Everyone has equal opportunity to profiting off OW causes. If it
is proven that any group or individual has profited from the system
without merit (i.e., without a contribution or against policy) then
coins can be returned during the holding period and/or legal action can
be taken.

### Always Evolving

Since OW is decentralized and based on smart contract, new or updated
policies that come out of causes will get community voted and integrated
into the decentralized system. The entire integration process will get
tracked on the blockchain, thus greatly minimizing any tampering or
manipulation of the systems policies and policy integration process. The
community can continue to create services that follow the OW principals,
to grow it into education, healthcare, all aspects of community managing
and growing a healthier society.

### Secure Identity

Identity is one of the great supporting pillars of the OW. Without
proper identification, users can make fake accounts, manipulate voting,
and perform untraceable actions. To achieve secure identity the
following will need to be implemented. Only referral accounts; Users can
only create accounts by being vetted with an invite by an existing
active account holder. Referring several banned accounts will have
repercussions.

### Privacy

The balance between privacy and security will be community driven as
everything else will be in the OW. All interactions with the OW can be
anonymous by users. Users can vote, post, add bounty all anonymously if
they wish. Users can always enable their identity on any previous
anonymous interactions if they like. This will be secured on the
blockchain and impossible to tamper with.

# Top Q & A from previous readers

1.  I still don't understand how Open World makes money?

    a.  It does not. Open World is merely a platform, it is not a
        profiting organization. Only contributors can make money through
        hard and smart work.

2.  Do bounty contributors get anything when the cause has a solution?
    As investors?

    a.  For the most part, no, contributing to a bounty is just a
        donation in order to make something happen. In some cases, a
        bounty can be raised through tokens or reservations with a
        minimal limit. E.g. \$300 bounty purchase reserves you a
        prototype phone made by a cause.

3.  How do companies get involved?

    a.  They can create a company account on the Open World and Interact
        with the Open World almost identically to a user, but they do
        not get votes. Companies can post, reply, add bounty, bid, etc.

4.  Where does money come from? Where is it held?

    a.  Money comes from users that believe in a cause or idea and want
        to make it happen. The funds are held in a secure decentralized
        blockchain that no one will have access to except for the bounty
        awarding smart contract.

5.  Won't there be many lost ideas and solutions by downvoting posts?

    a.  Yes, and this should be a natural and socially driven process.
        If it is a good idea, it will get attention. Campaigning a cause
        of course helps. Users can share a cause on any social media
        platform to help gain momentum.

6.  Regarding the coin bounty, at the end of the project(s) how can more
    coin be paid out than was originally contributed? What\'s the
    incentive for donating coin, time, money or expertise? How is value
    created that can then be distributed back to the contributors?

    a.  The bounty equals all of the coins that were funded, and so the
        coins paid out from the bounty will be exactly equal to the
        bounty. What goes in is what comes out, there is no middle man,
        no hidden generation or consumption of funds.

7.  Regarding the coin bounty, if someone decides to contribute by
    donating coin, who is responsible for the donation bounty and how it
    is spent on any particular project?

    a.  There is no human element involved in any part or process of the
        bounty, besides the initial donation. The distribution of the
        bounty is automatic as well as community voted.

8.  I'm still not sure how people would earn profit from their
    contributions, it all seems like its donation based? How is value
    created from the sum of its parts, how to pay out that benefit?

    a.  Users and society benefits from making helpful causes a reality,
        since it will always provide some better utility to society.
        Users can profit by replying with any post that contributes
        towards the cause. When the cause is successful, contributors
        will receive a portion of the bounty.
