# Why are we voting on people, When we could be voting on ideas?

I saw a recent meme "Trying to pick my favourite politician is like
trying to decide which STD is right for me." Although raw, it spoke to
me, touching on feelings and thoughts I have had for the past decade
with the current democratic system I have experienced.

I might like ideas from opposing sides, but I am forced to select one
side that has more of what I like, why? Why are we not collaborating and
working together instead of competing as representatives?

So why is it the way it is? Before the internet and modern
communication, the current democratic system was needed. We had no way
for majority of the population to have access to political proceedings
and information without all of them traveling long distances, to central
gatherings were the proceedings and information was held. Instead we
elected representatives for the people, to travel those distances and
propose new policies, vote or remove these policies on our behalf. This
was more economical then.

Now since the digital age, it is no longer the most economical option
and we are seeing great friction towards parties and representatives
through social media from the masses. A slow and subtle leaderless
socioeconomic uprising is occurring, and we are oblivious to it.

We now have the means for majority of society to take part directly in
political proceedings and information without needing to travel, from
the comfort of their home or smartphone. The political middleman in that
setup would no longer be needed. But we do not yet have the modern
system to facilitate such an change.

A system needs to emerge that will provide easy access to the masses to
not only vote on ideas, but create ideas collaboratively. The need for
representatives to propose, filter, and vote on ideas then becomes
inefficient compared to the proposed system.

1.  No representative means not having to pay them, for their campaigns,
    and other work related expenses that can amount to a great deal of
    tax dollar spent.

2.  Society will operate this system through passion towards change or
    an idea, not as a daily 9-5 job that they must complete each day.
    Passion as a motivator drives the greatest change, it is also free
    to taxpayers.

3.  Ideas can be implemented individually, catered to localities,
    instead of having a set of ideas that we must choose between between
    political parties.

4.  Thoundsands or millions of heads instead of hundreds, the resource
    available will be like never before.

5.  Natural scalability and incentives.

The existing government can then focus on implementing the top voted
ideas. These ideas will cost money to implement, and so government
officials can decide how to allocate funds. This would eventually become
a bottleneck for progress.

The next step would be to integrate funding into the idea creating and
voting system. If a voter finds value in a proposed idea, they not only
can vote for it, but they can help fund a bounty reward for making the
idea happen as a goal. When the idea is implemented, verified and voted
as completed by the community, all the funds in the bounty will be
transferred to the contributors.

This can be used for simple ideas and goals or complex causes.

At this point this system can allow users to propose ideas, vote, fund
and implement these ideas.

Let\'s look at a simple example. A traffic light does not seem to change
half the time. You pull up to a red, and want to make a left, but it
often skips your left advance. You are left waiting 5 or more minutes
for it to change. You create a post describing the problem. And create a
goal to fix the broken advance. You fund the goals bounty 3 dollars. You
can also make a print out, "Let\'s fix this left advance" with a qr code
that links to the post and tape it to the intersection like a garage
sale sign. You can also tag the post with a geo location so users can
find it easier.

After a few weeks, the bounty has grown to \$1500 and 6000 people have
upvoted it. Someone replies that they will be filing the paperwork to
fix the problem. A few weeks later someone notices the advanced light is
working and votes to award the bounty for the above response. They also
comment saying "seems fixed now". Someone else comments to the reply
saying "i saw workers there this morning, looks like it was fixed". A
few days later 3000 out of the 6000 initial post voters also vote to
award the bounty. The award vote passes and the bounty is transferred in
full to the account of the reply that won the award. There is a holding
period on the transfer for any disputes.

Human Factor

Everyone suffers from the same human condition: Emotions. They are great
for the individual human experience but not so great for making non
bias, objective decisions. Anyone that is making a decision which
affects another person has the risk of making a harmful, unjust or
unfair decision.

Human beings can be very creative and great problem solvers, especially
in collaborations.

# Scrap

Posts can contain other posts called replies

Replies can be made to the general post, or tagged to something
specific.

Posts can have functionality

A suggestion is a post that has functionality to replace content with
suggested content once it is voted in.

Suggestions can be made to any part of the post.
