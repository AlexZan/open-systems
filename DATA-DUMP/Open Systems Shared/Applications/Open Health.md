A global health system developed and maintained using the open systems
principals. This is not a core open system system

# Security

Using blockchain, patient data can be stored securely without risk of
tampering or hacking.

# Globally Accessible

The patient data will no longer be proprietary to a clinic, town, or
nation, it should be available globally with no restriction

# Open Standard

Using open principals, an evolving open standard for patient data
structure and protocol can be developed.

By using an open standard, many smart devices can feed data to our
profile, such as smart watches that monitor heart rate, blood oxygen,
smart blood pressure monitors, smart body scales, home air quality
monitors.

Data from existing labs and requisitions can be inputted into the
patients profile as well.

# Open Anonymous Data

Data should be accessible to all, anywhere, in order for studies or AI
to use the data to further help improve global health care and quality
of life.

# Early Warning and Detection

By having all the patient data in one place, as well as lab data and
smart device data, AI can look for learned patterns in early detection
of disease. As soon as it sees a notable amount of signals towards
possible diseases or conditions, it can notify the patient and doctor to
have it investigated further.

# Holistic Care

By connecting with devices and applications which track our good and
exercise, AI can also make suggestions on improving health. E.g. When
and what type of exercise to perform completely custom tailored to the
individual. What food to eat based off predicted nutritional
deficiencies,

Custom tailored care, diet plans, and exercise through optional genetic
data.

# Community Test Centers

Eventually community funded test centers can be established in order to
measure specific data required by doctors or predictive AI. e.g. A blood
lab in a developing area can be globally funded or locally funded.

# Crowd Source Response

Along with local first responders, a user in the area is able to receive
a response request for an emergency situation, if they opted in for such
a service. The request will depend on the emergency situation, and the
users certification for emergency response.

E.g. someone who has not been responsive through their smart watch
monitoring their heart rate and confirming it is intentional on their
smartphone will receive a response from anyone in the area with CPR
certification.

# Diagnostic

A patient can report all of the health issues that they are facing.
Through an incidence tracking app, they can report details on when the
issue occurred or changed. E.g. a migraine from 3am to 9pm next day,
pain level 7/10, etc.

AI will then try to find patterns between all reported incidences and
any data collected locally and from the user. E.g. a pattern was found
with a combination of high pollen activity in your area and coffee
drinking, that has a 90% chance to cause a migraine.
