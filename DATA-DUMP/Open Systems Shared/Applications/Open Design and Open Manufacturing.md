# Open Design and Open Manufacturing

Designs using Open World can be community collaborations (products a
community needs), global collaborations (products the world needs) or in
between. If you were to build a house, you could select from a wide
selection of top global created home designs ranging from all sizes all
styles. Then to build it, you would rent a community concrete printer,
load in the design, load the material and start printing the shell of
the home. Then you would complete the interior and final touches
yourself. If you are not a do it yourselfer then you would pay for an
construction open service, to build the home for you at cost. An open
service is an open source democratized blockchain service which the
community operates and has no ownership and no profit. Everything is
priced at cost. Everything is managed through an ever growing community
voted policy.

The concrete printer which would be used in a home building project
would have been a community project itself. Its design would be a global
collaboration. The first open source concrete printer would be designed
and any individual, group or company would then be able to pay a local
manufacturing open service to create the device for them. The payment
itself could be a group fund using the open world for community access
to a concrete printer.

Designs would be a natural iterative ever evolving process. There will
be the initial design, the first manufactured product, its various uses
globally and feedback, including what worked well, what didn\'t.
Designers would then fix problems and add upgrades to the design. The
latest design would then be manufactured. There would also be forks in
the design versions, where designs split into more specialized designs,
such as a 3d printer for factories versus one for homes, even though
they both have the same core and share some of the same parts. This is a
process built of the existing open source practices of today.

As you can see, production of goods from the initial design to
manufacturing and distribution, would be based fully off a natural
market governed by community demand. There would be no wasted funds on
marketing or advertising. There would be no wasted funds on corporate
theft, litigation and unhealthy competition. Ideally only designs and
ideas would compete, but not the individuals, groups or companies that
own the ideas, since there will be no owners.
