# Profile

When the user first registers, they will only need to provide an email
and password.

# Article

A user would like to create and post an article. They can create a draft
post and make it public, shareable by link, or viewable by specific
users, editable by specific users or make suggestions by specific users.
They can get more advanced and enable suggestions for users who have
more experience in some tag(s).

As they begin to compose the article, they can change the privacy level
at any time at no cost. Maybe when its ready for a first rough draft,
they can allow friends to see and leave suggestions, on the second rough
draft they can allow experts in the subject to do the same. Later on
they can make it shareable by link. Finally when its ready they can make
it full public.

Users at any time can donate to the article which would directly go to
the author. They can sponsor the user through the article.

Since the article has an original author, it should be accepted that as
long as the author is capable of maintaining the article, it will be
left private and not open. Although anyone can leave suggestions on the
article, it is up to the author to accept the suggestions.

Just like a regular post users can leave comments and reference the
article in any other post, or link any other post in the comments.

E.g if the article is used as motivation for a new project, the article
can be referenced in the project, and a response to the article can be
that with the project.

# First Post

A user goes to the open forum. They press the new post button. A forum
with title and body show. They are given a notification that since they
dont have an account, the post will be anonymous and they can not earn
experience. They can then create an account or continue.

If they continue, they can press the post button after filling out
content. Since the post is anonymous, they can not supply any tags. The
message will get posted, and anyone viewing the forum will see the new
post. Selecting the post from the list of posts brings up the post page.
The post will indicate it is from an anonymous account. A user can press
the upvote button on the post, and type in subject tag, since it was a
joke, they can use #comedy and #funny to upvote.
