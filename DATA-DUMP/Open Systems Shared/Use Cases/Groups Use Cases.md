# Use cases of decision that can't be handled by universal democracy.

1.  A group that has funding which needs spending

    a.  A community with tax backed project decisions. Alternative:
        raise funds for project options through open funding. What if
        someone outside the community raises the funds, but the
        community does not want the project. This is essentially voting
        with money.

2.  A decision which has no public value. E.g. should our team shorts be
    yellow or blue?

Even a decision that only affects a group can still benefit from the
public weighing in on it. Even if the vote is group only, the discussion
can be public.

Community example: a community wants to fund something that not all the
community agrees with, but one very wealthy donor reaches the funding
goal.

1.  If it is within the legal in the locality to build the project, then
    they cant be stopped, as much as you can stop someone from building
    anything they like that is legal on their private property. Open
    World does make it any more possible. More so, users can voice their
    opinions against project and if there is enough support for it, the

    getting something majority of the community wants.

Damn example. Town A wants to fund a damn in oder to provide hydro
electricity to the town. But town B will have its valley flooded by this
town. This project not only affects town A but town B.

Carbon example: A big carbon producing project is getting funded in
country A, but all other countries will suffer the global rise in
emissions as a result, this decision involves everyone on earth.

# Soccer group

5 person soccer group. Everyone knows each other in person. If a fake
user is added they will know right away since it\'s a small local group
they will know there is no new physical person.

New member

1.  A user invites the new member and provides them with a verification
    token

2.  The user consumes the verification token when requesting to join

3.  Up to two more existing members are selected at random to provide
    the potential new member with two more consumable verification
    tokens.

4.  After all verification tokens are accepted, they join the group.

Deciding what field to play on

1.  A vote is made by a user

2.  Whenever an option reaches \>50% of the total votes, the vote passes
    and ends.
