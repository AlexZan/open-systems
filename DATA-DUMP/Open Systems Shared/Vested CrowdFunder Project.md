Similar to popular crowdfunding platforms with several exceptions:

1.  Users can fund projects for tokens. Each token provides one voting
    right.

2.  Projects can be broken down into objectives recursively

3.  Users can vote if an objective passes or fails

How do you stop project owners voting to buy back? A project owner can
create a fake account, and purchase majority voting tokens essentially
for free. This can provide the impression of a consensus but it will be
influenced or taken over by the owners voting. An owner can create
multiple fake accounts to make it less obvious.

One way to combat this is not to make it cost. If an owner wishes to
purchase votes, they will lose 10-30% of the cost.

Another option is to make interaction weight. So if a user has no
interaction, their voting tokens are worth 0 in votes. What is
considered interaction can also be faked, so this does not stop the
problem just makes it harder.

Peer authenticate. A user can only vote if they peer authenticate. They
must request it. Another user will be asked to scan the user\'s qr code
in person. Because the person scanning "the scanner" was picked at
random, we know they can not be an actor. The scanner validates that
they are in fact a real person.

How does this stop the same person from validating a different, since
the scanner is random. It does not. This method only validates that they
are a human, but not that they only have a single account registered to
them.

This could be solved by tracking signature biometrics, but that is
intrusive.

Tracking interactions. Tracking interactions with other members and
organizations in the physical space would increase a weight of trust and
time for that user. But this would require the platform to be widely
used.

Another option is a penalty.

Need an intermediary solution prior to Open Democracy.

The solution is somewhat naturally occurring. Since the owner will need
to buy more than half the votes to control it, they will need to front
half of what they think they will raise, and even then they might raise
more and lose the majority. I believe this will be a significant
deterrent for someone trying to commit fraud but requiring half up front
of what they will get out. Furthermore, if the backers realize their
votes and concerns are being ignored, for the first objective, they will
pull out their funds for the rest. If the fraudster waits until the last
objective to commit the fake votes, well by then they will already have
needed the majority of the project finished for the backers to stay on
board. Unless it was all fake and they were all duped, in which case a
local police investigation would take place. The idea is for it not to
be a pattern.

Maybe the platform keeps 1-10% of the funds from the first tokens
deposited. That way if the owner is the first to do it, they will not
see any of those funds.

## Credit Growth Rough Idea

If a user contributed \$100 to the project early on, and later on it is
profitable and repays all its debts, then any extra profit will go to
the non debt profit split. Maybe factoring in time plus amount, each
backer gets a return from the ongoing profit.

Lets say 1 million was raised for development, and the project is now
generating 1 million a year in profit. Then this one million annually is
redistributed to all the existing token holders. Maybe 20-50% is in the
owner group, so they are earning 200-500k a year. And the rest goes to
the backers and contributors.

If the owner creates 10 million tokens, and puts up 5 million for sale
at 10 dollars each.

Later investors will get less vote per \$ or contribution

Essentially this sets up nearly a ponzi scheme, except that the new
backers are gaining the value of the product and thus it is a legitimate
exchange, but, the old backers are getting all the profit value from the
new backers. Another way to think of it, is that every consumer can be
an investor over time, and earn profit from the consumers that maybe
also become investors.

If the backer token which provides a vote has a limited supply then at
some point it will exhausted, and then only holders can supply tokens at
a higher cost.

Though, maybe, since there is a limited supply, not all consumers can be
backers. Therefor the ponzi scheme concept fails with this in mind.
Consumers will just be consumers, and if they wish to purchase backer
tokens that are available they can be backers also, but it is not
automatic. The product supply must be unlimited, yet the backer token
supply must be limited.

A backer that has faith in the operation will continue to hold their
tokens for their profit share, while a backer that is losing faith,
might sell their tokens on the market.

The idea is that backers will earn a profit split proportional to their
token holding, as well as have voting power proportional to it. The
earlier they purchase them, the cheaper they can get them for.

The problem with this again, is that if there is a limited supply then,
any new buyer, who is using the product, who should have a say in its
direction will not be able to have a say. Therefore votes should have an
endless supply, the vote should include all vested users.

An idea is to give voting power to only those users who receive tokens
for creating value. Since they have created value in the past, they are
likely to continue to do it, and thus should be in power of decisions.
Maybe they should be half of the decision making power, or some fraction
of it, compared to backers, since backers should also have a say.

Maybe backers can vote to change that ratio, or to make it fully in the
control of value creators, and only backers get to make this vote.

### Profit tokens vs vote tokens

Profit tokens should have a limited supply to create growth through
scarcity. While vote tokens should be unlimited, since backers,
contributors and consumers are all affected by decisions made, therefore
they should all have a say.

The progress in a project should see the founders and backers have most
say initially, then as it develops, contributors begin to have a say,
then as it enters the market, the consumers would have a greater say,
maybe eventually, taking majority.

Should investors continue to receive unlimited supply of return from
profit? Or should it be more like bonds where they are promised a return
on profit, and once that return and debit is repaid, it ends there. Then
who continues to get the profit? Well if it is an open venture, then, no
one does, and the cost should eventually perfectly match the expenses of
operation. But then this implies that early consumers backers paid more
for the product since they covered the cost of development. Should there
be compensation for this? Maybe, but should it be without limit? It is
more so the intellectual value that was created, which should have
unlimited return. Maybe all of these are voted by the community. As
consumers take over and are the majority, they have final say to where
their money goes. The end result could be 80% operations, 10% backers,
10% intellectual contributors. So it probably should be indefinite, but
the majority will dictate what the splits are.

One effect is that as the project continues to improve, more and more
contributors will provide value for it to improve, and thus they will
earn a profit split, so the contributor split will be divided against
more and more individuals, providing less value at the current profit
rate. The idea is that as the value grows, so should the profit, and
thus the split value for each contributor. This should balance itself,
in that, if the split value is so small, it will not attract valuable
contributions. Contributors might go to projects that have better
earnings. In this case the majority would increase the split to
contributors in order to attract contributors to progress the project
with the communities request improvements.

## Funding to Production Transition

The platform should support the transition from the funding stage, and
into the production, normal operation, stage. The purchases made in
production are made with a different agreement than the funding
agreement. Each production stage funds the next, and can only pass into
the next phase when the consensus of the previous phase allows it to.

### General

1.  Create project draft with multiple objectives

2.  Share with people, get feedback, get subscribers

3.  Launch project, mint tokens, lock in objectives

4.  Users make an exchange for the tokens (purchase token)

    a.  Program to handle transfer on purchase, hold funds.

        i.  The cost will be specified in fiat, therefore the crypo/sol
            cost will need to be updated at the time of transaction with
            the current exchange usd/crypto(x).

5.  Owner submits proof of completion

6.  Users vote with each token whether they approve or reject the
    submission (rejection must have reason/id)

7.  Once the approval consensus threshold is met, the funds of the next
    objective are transferred to the owners account, the rest remain
    locked in the project account.

8.  If the approval is rejected, the funds remain, and the owner can
    make the required fixes and make another submission.

9.  Once the final objective passes, production will begin and funding
    for a batch of tokens will be delivered if only the Q&A from the
    previous batch passes.
