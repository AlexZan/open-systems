## Expressive Politics as a Propagation Algorithm

The propagation of **expressive politics** can be modeled similarly to how **national identity** forms and self-organizes across populations. Both arise not from top-down directives but from **emergent, self-reinforcing alignment mechanisms**. These mechanisms can be abstracted into an algorithmic model describing how collective expression stabilizes into boundaries of shared identity or preference.

### National Identity Propagation

In national identity, propagation follows natural social dynamics:

- **Affinity Clustering** — individuals imitate and adopt narratives close to their cultural, linguistic, or value neighbors.  
- **Boundary Formation** — once cohesion exceeds a threshold, contrast to external groups reinforces the shared identity.  
- **Feedback Stabilization** — shared symbols, rituals, and recognition loops (e.g., flags, languages) maintain internal coherence.

### Expressive Politics Propagation

In **expressive politics**, the same dynamics apply, but without centralized authority or fixed ideological borders:

- **Local Preference Averaging** — individuals align their expressed values based on local or social overlap (affinity weighting).  
- **Emergent Cohesion Thresholds** — agreement on an issue defines a political “cluster.”  
- **Dynamic Boundary Reinforcement** — contrast with other clusters strengthens identity, but boundaries remain fluid and data-driven.  
- **Cultural Diffusion** — individuals at intersections act as bridges transferring values between clusters.

### The Algorithm of Expressive Politics

1. Each participant broadcasts a **multidimensional vector** of expressed values.  
2. Individuals locally align their vectors based on overlapping affinity.  
3. Clusters of high internal alignment and strong contrast to others naturally emerge — forming expressive boundaries.  
4. Cohesion within each cluster becomes a measurable signal (similar to “national identity strength”), defining influence weight in collective decision models.

### Summary

**Expressive politics is national identity without geography.**  
It represents a **fluid, data-driven field** of self-organizing affinities rather than fixed territorial lines. This concept aligns with the **Open Democracy** framework, where individuals *express* rather than *vote*, and where **cohesion metrics** replace binary majority outcomes.
