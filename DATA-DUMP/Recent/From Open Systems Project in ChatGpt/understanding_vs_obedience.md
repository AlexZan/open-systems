In society, an individual can only function through one of two modes: understanding or obedience.

1. **Understanding**
   Acting through understanding means grasping the reasoning, implications, and context behind an action or decision. The individual takes responsibility for their own comprehension and choices. This is the basis of autonomy and sovereignty.

2. **Obedience**
   Acting through obedience means following an order, rule, or influence without full comprehension. The decision to act is delegated to another, whether through trust, fear, or convenience. This is the foundation of hierarchy and dependency.

This dichotomy mirrors the contrast described in *Open Democracy*:
- In representative or direct democracies, most individuals cannot fully understand every issue, so they follow influencers or experts. Though the system appears participatory, it is functionally obedience.
- In open systems, transparent access to information and reasoned discussion aim to increase understanding, gradually reducing the reliance on obedience.

**Principle:** Obedience is the fallback when understanding is absent.

**Corollary:** True sovereignty emerges only when understanding is both possible and chosen.

This aligns with the freedom proof: if a community chooses to be controlled, that choice contradicts freedom unless its members understand the consequences. Therefore, understanding is not just a path to freedom—it is freedom itself.
